5万台。

这是特斯拉第三代Optimus 2026年的目标产量。7月下旬，加州工厂将正式启动量产。零部件超过1万个，盟立集团已供应谐波减速器和关节模组，亚洲光学供应高分辨率镜头（单台机器人配7-8个镜头用于视觉辨识和手部感知）。

马斯克说Optimus未来将贡献特斯拉80%的价值。2025年Optimus生产了5000台，2026年目标是5万台——10倍增长。未来年产能规划1000万台。

这不是PPT，不是Demo视频，不是舞台上的表演——这是量产。2026年，被全行业定义为人形机器人的"十万台级量产元年"。

资本用脚投票：上半年460亿涌入

钱不会骗人。据IT桔子统计，2026年上半年国内具身智能及机器人领域共发生288起融资事件，涉及226家企业，披露融资额超460亿元。

千寻智能三个月内完成四轮融资累计45亿元，估值突破200亿元，其自研Spirit v1.6模型在RoboArena榜单上反超英伟达Cosmos 3登顶全球第一。创始人韩峰涛的判断很明确："未来决定机器人能力上限的是模型，核心竞争资源不是算力而是真实世界数据。"

无界动力更猛——成立仅一年即完成超2亿美元天使轮融资，Pre-A轮近2亿美元也接近完成，已签署近1亿美元订单，第二代机器人K15已开启批量投产。创始人张玉峰曾任地平线高管，走"隐空间世界模型"路线。德国人形机器人公司Neura Robotics获最高14亿美元C轮，由英伟达、亚马逊参投。

港大教授李弘扬创立的源策未来成立仅两个月即获真格、高榕、IDG、五源数亿元种子轮。宇树科技从科创板受理到过会仅73天，计划募资42.02亿元，预计三季度挂牌。

价格雪崩：从百万到万元

量产带来的最直接变化是价格。

就在两年前，一台人形机器人的成本还在百万级别。2026年，人形机器人单价已经从百万元级跌至十万甚至万元级别。优必选消费级人形机器人"优世界"预售突破5000台，智元机器人开启"机器人进厂打工"6天直播，展示3C电子产线上料/分拣/组装实操。

西门子展示了更令人兴奋的场景：具身智能机器人未经编程即完成杂物分拣，称"工厂正从自动化走向自适应"。这意味着机器人不再需要精确的预编程来执行固定任务——它可以像人类工人一样，通过观察和学习来适应新的工作环境。

世界模型成为关键技术路径。英伟达推出开源全模态物理AI世界模型Cosmos 3，腾讯发布混元3D世界模型2.0，阿里推出HappyOyster，吉利发布WAM世界行为模型，小鹏推出X-World。华为云6月5日发布全球首个全流程具身智能开发平台CloudRobo公测。世界模型被视为通向具身AGI的关键路径——让机器人在虚拟世界中学习，然后迁移到物理世界执行。

"投资逻辑已经从'看团队、看Demo'转向'看交付、看数据闭环'。能在工厂里连续干活不翻车的机器人，比舞台上后空翻的机器人值钱一百倍。" —— 一位具身智能领域投资人总结

从展厅到车间的最后一公里

但需要清醒地看到，量产≠普及。

目前人形机器人的主要应用场景仍然是B端工业场景——3C制造、汽车产线、物流仓储。C端消费级市场（家庭保姆、陪伴机器人）距离成熟还有至少3-5年。硬件成本降下来了，但可靠性、安全性、通用性的问题远没有完全解决。

工信部与国务院国资委联合启动实景实训专项行动，要求2026年底形成百个以上高价值具身智能应用场景，带动万台级规模落地。北京具身智能机器人超级工厂预计2030年产能达50万台套。

从实验室到工厂，从工厂到家庭——人形机器人的产业化路径正在一步步清晰。2026年，是从"能不能做"到"能不能量产"的拐点。接下来的问题是：量产之后，能不能真正解决问题？

50,000 units.

That's Tesla's production target for the third-generation Optimus in 2026. In late July, the California factory will officially start mass production. With over 10,000 components, alliance partners have already supplied harmonic reducers and joint modules; Asia Optical supplies high-resolution lenses (each robot equipped with 7-8 lenses for visual recognition and hand perception).

Musk says Optimus will contribute 80% of Tesla's future value. In 2025, Optimus produced 5,000 units; the 2026 target is 50,000 -- a 10x increase. Future annual capacity is planned at 10 million units.

This isn't a PPT, a demo video, or stage performance -- this is mass production. 2026 has been defined across the industry as the "year of 100K-unit mass production" for humanoid robots.

Capital Votes With Its Feet: 46 Billion Pours In During H1

Money doesn't lie. According to IT Orange statistics, in H1 2026, domestic embodied AI and robotics saw 288 funding events involving 226 companies, with disclosed funding exceeding 46 billion RMB.

Qianxun Intelligence completed four rounds totaling 4.5 billion RMB within three months, valuation breaking 20 billion; its self-developed Spirit v1.6 model overtook Nvidia Cosmos 3 to claim #1 globally on the RoboArena leaderboard. Founder Han Fengtao's judgment is clear: "The future ceiling of robot capabilities will be determined by models; the core competitive resource isn't compute but real-world data."

Wujie Dynamics is even more aggressive -- just one year after founding, it completed a $200M+ angel round, with a nearly $200M Pre-A close, nearly $100M in signed orders, and second-generation robot K15 already entering batch production. Founder Zhang Yufeng, formerly a Horizon Robotics executive, is pursuing a "latent-space world model" approach. German humanoid robot company Neura Robotics secured a C round of up to $1.4B, with Nvidia and Amazon participating.

HKU professor Li Hongyang's Yuance Future raised hundreds of millions in seed funding from ZhenFund, Gaorong, IDG, and 5Y Capital just two months after launch. Unitree Robotics went from STAR Market acceptance to approval in just 73 days, planning to raise 4.202 billion RMB with an expected Q3 listing.

Price Collapse: From Millions to Five Figures

The most direct change from mass production is price.

Just two years ago, the cost of a humanoid robot was still in the million-yuan range. In 2026, humanoid robot unit prices have dropped from millions to the 100K or even 10K yuan range. UBTECH's consumer-grade humanoid robot "U-World" surpassed 5,000 pre-orders; Agibot launched a 6-day livestream of "robots going to work in factories," demonstrating material handling/sorting/assembly on 3C electronics production lines.

Siemens showcased something more exciting: embodied AI robots sorting miscellaneous items without programming, declaring that "factories are moving from automation to self-adaptation." This means robots no longer need precise pre-programming to execute fixed tasks -- they can adapt to new work environments through observation and learning, just like human workers.

World models are becoming the key technical path. Nvidia launched the open-source full-modal physical AI world model Cosmos 3; Tencent released Hunyuan 3D World Model 2.0; Alibaba launched HappyOyster; Geely released WAM World Action Model; Xpeng launched X-World. Huawei Cloud released CloudRobo, the world's first end-to-end embodied AI development platform, in public beta on June 5. World models are seen as the key path to embodied AGI -- letting robots learn in virtual worlds, then transfer to physical execution.

"The investment thesis has shifted from 'look at the team, look at the demo' to 'look at delivery, look at the data loop.' A robot that can work continuously in a factory without screwing up is a hundred times more valuable than a robot doing backflips on stage." -- An embodied AI investor's summary

The Last Mile From Showroom to Workshop

But a clear-eyed view is essential: mass production doesn't equal widespread adoption.

Currently, the main application scenarios for humanoid robots remain B2B industrial scenarios -- 3C manufacturing, automotive production lines, logistics and warehousing. The C-end consumer market (home helpers, companion robots) is at least 3-5 years from maturity. Hardware costs have come down, but reliability, safety, and versatility issues are far from fully resolved.

The Ministry of Industry and Information Technology and SASAC jointly launched a real-scenario training initiative, requiring the formation of over 100 high-value embodied AI application scenarios by end of 2026, driving 10,000-unit scale deployment. Beijing's embodied AI robot super-factory targets 500,000 units annual capacity by 2030.

From lab to factory, from factory to home -- the industrialization path for humanoid robots is becoming clearer step by step. 2026 is the inflection point from "can we build it" to "can we mass-produce it." The next question is: after mass production, can they actually solve problems?
