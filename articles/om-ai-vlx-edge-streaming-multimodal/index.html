0.06秒。

这是VLX-Flow处理单路视频流的最低延迟。对人类来说，0.06秒大约是一次眨眼时间的三分之一。在这个时间内，模型完成了对新一帧画面的理解、更新对环境的认知、判断是否需要做出反应。

7月1日，Om AI联汇发布VLX系列——全球首个面向物理世界的端侧流式多模态模型。它做了一件此前所有视觉多模态模型都没做到的事：不是“看完一段视频再回答”，而是“边看边理解、必要时主动行动”。

从“截帧看图”到“流式感知”

现有的视频理解模型是怎么工作的？把视频切成一帧一帧的图片，送进模型做离线处理，然后输出一个结果。这就像一个人看世界时每隔几秒睁开一次眼、每次看一张照片，然后闭着眼分析照片——你可以想象，这样感知世界会非常笨拙，无法应对动态变化的环境。

VLX的思路完全不同。它提出了“流式多模态”架构：视觉信息以连续流的方式持续进入模型，通过增量编码和缓存推理机制，模型像人一样持续观察环境，新画面随时吸收，提问瞬间响应。这对应的不是“更好的视频问答体验”，而是AI自主工作能力的质变。

VLX系列由三个模型组成：VLX-Flow负责持续感知（增量编码+缓存推理），VLX-Seek负责精准定位（把坐标生成变成区域检索，不是“猜坐标”而是“选区域”），VLX-Go负责行动执行（把视觉理解直接转化为机器人可执行的运动轨迹，不是输出文字建议）。三者协同，在端侧设备上跑通了“持续感知→精准定位→行动决策”的完整闭环。

模型规格覆盖0.6B到10B参数——不是把云端大模型压缩塞进终端，而是从架构层面为端侧具身智能重新设计。

物理AI的2026年中时刻

年初CES上，黄仁勋在演讲中17次提及“物理AI”，宣告“物理AI的ChatGPT时刻已经来了”。半年过去，这个领域的信号密度确实在爆发。

融资端：前百度自动驾驶与机器人实验室主任杨睿刚博士创办的纽娲机器人，成立仅4个月就完成种子+天使两轮共5000万元融资；苏州大学刘瑞远教授创办的感知纪元，做机器人多模态电子皮肤，获松禾资本千万级天使轮；优艾智合发布具身智能系列新品，目标3年赋能10000个工业现场。Wayve（自动驾驶AI）以85亿美元估值启动8500万美元员工股权回购。上半年国内具身智能领域288起融资事件、超460亿元融资额。

技术端：智元机器人第15000台通用具身机器人精灵G2量产下线；宇树科技从受理到过会仅73天创下科创板纪录。36氪的报道指出，物理AI在VLA模型、世界模型、训练场、本体及商业闭环五个维度都取得了关键进展。

但需要冷静。物理AI的“ChatGPT时刻”还没有真正到来。机器人在受控环境（工厂、仓库）中的表现越来越好，但在开放环境中的泛化能力仍然有限。触觉、力觉、运动控制等“身体”层面的技术，比“大脑”（VLA模型）的进展要慢。VLX解决的是“眼睛和大脑”的问题，但机器人还需要“手”和“脚”的成熟。

不过方向是明确的。当AI从屏幕走向物理世界，“流式感知”是一个架构层面的范式革新——它让AI开始像生物一样，用连续的时间维度去理解世界，而不是用离散的截图。这是物理AI从demo走向产品的必经之路。

明天见。

0.06 seconds.

That's VLX-Flow's minimum latency for processing a single video stream. For humans, 0.06 seconds is roughly one-third of a blink. In that time, the model completes understanding a new frame, updating its environmental awareness, and judging whether a reaction is needed.

On July 1, Om AI released the VLX series -- the world's first edge streaming multimodal model for the physical world. It did something no previous vision multimodal model had done: instead of "watch a video clip then answer," it "understands while watching, and proactively acts when necessary."

From "Frame-by-Frame Screenshots" to "Streaming Perception"

How do existing video understanding models work? They slice video into frame-by-frame images, feed them into the model for offline processing, then output a result. That's like a person who opens their eyes every few seconds, looks at one photo each time, then closes their eyes to analyze it -- you can imagine how clumsy that perception would be, unable to cope with dynamically changing environments.

VLX's approach is fundamentally different. It proposes a "streaming multimodal" architecture: visual information enters the model as a continuous flow. Through incremental encoding and cached inference mechanisms, the model continuously observes the environment like a human, absorbing new frames as they arrive and responding instantly when queried. This doesn't correspond to a "better video Q&A experience" -- it's a qualitative leap in AI's ability to work autonomously.

The VLX series consists of three models: VLX-Flow handles continuous perception (incremental encoding + cached inference), VLX-Seek handles precise localization (turning coordinate generation into region retrieval -- not "guessing coordinates" but "selecting regions"), and VLX-Go handles action execution (converting visual understanding directly into robot-executable motion trajectories, not outputting text suggestions). The three work together to close the full loop of "continuous perception -> precise localization -> action decision" on edge devices.

Model sizes range from 0.6B to 10B parameters -- not cloud-scale models compressed onto edge devices, but redesigned from the architecture level for edge embodied AI.

Physical AI at Mid-2026

At CES earlier this year, Jensen Huang mentioned "physical AI" 17 times in his keynote, declaring that "physical AI's ChatGPT moment has arrived." Half a year later, signal density in this field is indeed exploding.

On the funding side: Nuwa Robotics, founded by former Baidu Autonomous Driving and Robotics Lab director Dr. Yang Ruigang, closed 50 million RMB across seed and angel rounds just 4 months after founding; Ganzhi Era, founded by Suzhou University Professor Liu Ruiyuan making multimodal electronic skin for robots, raised a 10-million-level angel round from Songhe Capital; Youai Zhihe released new embodied AI products targeting 10,000 industrial sites empowered in 3 years. Wayve (autonomous driving AI) initiated an $85 million employee share buyback at an $8.5 billion valuation. In the first half of the year, China's embodied AI sector saw 288 funding events totaling over 46 billion RMB.

On the technology side: AgiBot's 15,000th general embodied robot, the Genie G2, rolled off the production line; Unitree went from filing to IPO approval in a record 73 days on the STAR Market. As 36Kr reported, physical AI has made key progress across five dimensions: VLA models, world models, training grounds, hardware, and commercial closed loops.

"Whoever can digitize the physical world with high quality holds the entry point to the next phase of machine intelligence." -- Ledong Robotics on the physical AI赛道

But cool heads are needed. Physical AI's "ChatGPT moment" hasn't truly arrived yet. Robots perform increasingly well in controlled environments (factories, warehouses) but generalization in open environments remains limited. "Body"-level technologies like tactile sensing, force sensing, and motion control are progressing slower than the "brain" (VLA models). VLX solves the "eyes and brain" problem, but robots still need mature "hands" and "feet."

But the direction is clear. As AI moves from screens into the physical world, "streaming perception" is an architectural paradigm shift -- it lets AI start understanding the world through a continuous temporal dimension like biological organisms, rather than through discrete screenshots. This is the necessary path for physical AI to go from demo to product.
