7月22日，两个消息同时从OpenAI传出：ads.openai.com广告平台正式上线，同时推出ChatGPT for Small Business小企业扶持项目。前者在Hacker News上冲到600分、435条评论，热榜第一。

这两个消息放在一起看，信号非常清晰：OpenAI的商业化进入了新阶段。

广告上线意味着什么

科技公司的商业化路径有一个经典规律：当一家公司开始卖广告，说明它的核心增长故事已经讲完了。

Google在搜索份额见顶后把AdSense做成了最大广告网络；Meta在用户增长停滞后把News Feed广告变成了印钞机。现在轮到OpenAI了。

ChatGPT的月活已经停留在某个量级一段时间了，企业版（ChatGPT Work）的渗透速度可能低于预期，API收入面临Anthropic和开源模型的价格战压力。广告是最直接、最快的现金流来源——不需要复杂的销售周期，不需要企业部署，只要有流量就能变现。

但广告也是一个分水岭。它意味着ChatGPT从一个"你花钱买的生产力工具"变成了一个"免费使用但你是产品"的媒体平台。这对用户信任的影响是长期的：当你的AI助手在回答问题时夹杂广告，你还能完全信任它的答案吗？

小企业项目：下沉市场的信号

同一天发布的ChatGPT for Small Business项目则是另一个方向的信号：OpenAI开始向下沉市场要增长。

这个项目帮助小企业主学习AI技能、自动化工作、用ChatGPT Work发展业务。本质上是把原本面向大企业的销售体系，开始向SMB（中小企业）市场延伸。

大企业客户的特点是客单价高、决策周期长、 churn低；小企业客户的特点是客单价低、决策快、churn高但量大。当一家ToB公司开始大力做SMB市场，通常意味着两个原因：要么大企业市场渗透得差不多了，要么投资人要求增长故事继续。

结合广告平台来看，更可能是两者兼有。

AI商业化的三个阶段

OpenAI的路径，其实是所有AI公司都会走的路：

第一阶段：技术崇拜期。发布惊艳的模型，全世界围观，融资估值飙升，企业愿意为了"不落后"买单。这个阶段卖的是恐惧和希望。

第二阶段：工具落地期。推出具体产品（ChatGPT Work、API、行业解决方案），证明ROI，企业开始真正部署，个人用户订阅增长。这个阶段卖的是效率。

第三阶段：流量变现期。增长见顶，开始榨取现有用户的每一分价值：广告、增值服务、抽佣、生态税。这个阶段卖的是注意力。

OpenAI现在一只脚已经踏进了第三阶段。这不是坏事——一家公司最终需要赚钱才能持续投入研发。但这标志着一个时代的结束：那个"改变世界"的OpenAI，正在变成一家普通的科技公司。

对于AI创业者来说，这也是一个信号：当巨头开始收割，意味着市场进入成熟期，创业机会从"做模型"转向"做应用""做垂直""做服务"。

AI的夏天还没结束，但已经从"所有人都在讲故事"变成了"所有人都在算账"。

明天见。

On July 22, two announcements came from OpenAI simultaneously: the ads.openai.com advertising platform officially went live, alongside the launch of the ChatGPT for Small Business program. The former hit #1 on Hacker News with 600 points and 435 comments.

Taken together, these two messages send a clear signal: OpenAI's commercialization is entering a new phase.

What Advertising Means

There's a classic pattern in tech company monetization: when a company starts selling ads, its core growth story is over.

Google built AdSense into the world's largest ad network after search share peaked. Meta turned News Feed ads into a money printer after user growth stalled. Now it's OpenAI's turn.

ChatGPT's MAUs have plateaued for some time. Enterprise (ChatGPT Work) penetration may be slower than expected. API revenue faces price pressure from Anthropic and open-source models. Advertising is the most direct, fastest source of cash flow — no long sales cycles, no enterprise deployments, just traffic to monetize.

But advertising is also a watershed. It means ChatGPT shifts from being "a productivity tool you pay for" to "a media platform that's free because you are the product." The long-term impact on user trust is significant: when your AI assistant mixes ads into answers, can you fully trust what it says?

The Small Business Program: A Downmarket Signal

The ChatGPT for Small Business program, released the same day, is a signal in the other direction: OpenAI is going downmarket for growth.

The program helps entrepreneurs build AI skills, automate work, and grow their businesses using ChatGPT Work. Essentially extending what was an enterprise-focused sales motion into the SMB market.

Large enterprise clients have high ACV, long sales cycles, low churn. Small business clients have low ACV, quick decisions, high churn but volume. When a B2B company pushes hard into SMB, it usually means one of two things: either enterprise penetration is saturating, or investors are demanding the growth story continue.

Combined with the ad platform, it's likely both.

The Three Phases of AI Commercialization

OpenAI's path is one every AI company will walk:

Phase 1: Tech Worship.Release an amazing model, the world watches, valuations soar, enterprises pay to "not fall behind." This phase sells fear and hope.

Phase 2: Tool Deployment.Launch concrete products (ChatGPT Work, APIs, industry solutions), prove ROI, real deployments happen, individual subscriptions grow. This phase sells efficiency.

Phase 3: Attention Harvest.Growth peaks. Extract every dollar from existing users: ads, premium services, commissions, ecosystem taxes. This phase sells attention.

OpenAI now has one foot firmly in Phase 3. That's not inherently bad — a company ultimately needs to make money to fund R&D. But it marks the end of an era: the OpenAI that was "going to change the world" is becoming just another tech company.

For AI founders, this is also a signal: when the giants start harvesting, the market is maturing. Startup opportunities shift from "building models" to "building applications," "building verticals," "building services."

AI's summer isn't over, but it has shifted from "everyone telling stories" to "everyone running the numbers."

See you tomorrow.
