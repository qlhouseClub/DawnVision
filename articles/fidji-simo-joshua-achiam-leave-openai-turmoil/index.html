7月9日，两条消息同时传出：OpenAI应用业务负责人、内部仅次于Sam Altman的二号人物Fidji Simo宣布因长期慢性神经免疫系统疾病恶化，离开全职岗位转任兼职顾问；同日，OpenAI首席未来学家Joshua Achiam在X平台宣布将于本月底离职，结束在OpenAI近九年的职业生涯。

两位关键人物，两种不同信号

Simo和Achiam的离职性质完全不同，但叠加在一起释放的信号足够强烈。

Fidji Simo是Meta出身的产品高管，2024年初加入OpenAI负责应用业务，直接向Sam Altman汇报，是OpenAI从"研究实验室"转向"产品公司"的关键推手。她主导了ChatGPT的产品化、Sora的发布（以及后来的战略调整）、Cowork企业产品线的构建。Simo在内部信中明确表示，离职是因为三个月前开始休病假，神经免疫系统疾病的恢复比预期慢，经过慎重考虑决定将健康放在优先位置，转任兼职顾问后仍会参与关键产品决策。

Joshua Achiam的离职则更值得玩味。作为"首席未来学家"（Chief Futurist），Achiam在OpenAI内部是一个特殊角色——他的工作介于AI安全研究、政策制定和公司使命之间，负责研究AI的长期影响和存在性风险。他是2017年就加入OpenAI的元老级员工，见证了公司从非营利安全研究机构到千亿美元商业巨头的完整蜕变。Achiam在X上发文宣布离职时，没有明确说明下一步去向，但熟悉OpenAI内部情况的观察者指出，这是安全派系持续流失的又一案例。

安全派的"出埃及记"

把时间线拉长来看，Achiam的离职不是孤立事件。

2024年，联合创始人兼首席科学家Ilya Sutskever离开OpenAI创立Safe Superintelligence Inc.（SSI），带走了一批核心研究员；2025年，对齐团队负责人Jan Leike辞职投奔Anthropic，公开批评OpenAI"安全文化已死"；2026年以来，又有数名安全和对齐方向的资深研究员相继离开。Achiam的离开，让这条人才流失的线索更加清晰：OpenAI的"安全派"正在系统性地流失，留下来的更多是"加速派"——那些相信快速迭代和商业化才是通往AGI正途的人。

这种人才结构的变化会深刻影响OpenAI的产品和战略走向。Sima的离职虽然是健康原因，但时机非常微妙——GPT-5.6发布当天宣布、公司考虑IPO的关键节点、与微软关系出现裂痕的传闻期——二号人物的离开不可避免地会在短期内造成产品战略的真空。不过OpenAI选择在同一天发布GPT-5.6，也在传递一个信号：不管谁走，产品不会停。

明天见。

The applause for GPT-5.6 hadn't even faded before OpenAI's leadership earthquake struck again.

On July 9, two pieces of news broke simultaneously: Fidji Simo, OpenAI's head of applied business and the company's number-two executive behind Sam Altman, announced she's stepping down from her full-time role to become a part-time advisor due to a worsening chronic neuroimmune condition; the same day, OpenAI chief futurist Joshua Achiam announced on X that he's leaving at the end of the month, concluding a nearly nine-year career at the company.

Two Key Figures, Two Different Signals

Simo's and Achiam's departures are entirely different in nature, but their combination sends a powerful signal.

Fidji Simo is a Meta-trained product executive who joined OpenAI in early 2024 to lead applied business, reporting directly to Sam Altman. She was a key driver of OpenAI's pivot from "research lab" to "product company," spearheading ChatGPT productization, the Sora launch (and later strategic pivot), and building the Cowork enterprise product line. Simo made clear in her internal memo that the departure is due to medical leave that began three months ago; recovery from the neuroimmune condition has been slower than expected, and after careful consideration she's prioritizing her health, moving to an advisory role where she'll still participate in key product decisions.

Joshua Achiam's departure is more loaded. As "Chief Futurist," Achiam occupied a unique role at OpenAI — his work sat at the intersection of AI safety research, policy, and corporate mission, studying the long-term impacts and existential risks of AI. An early employee who joined in 2017, he witnessed the company's full transformation from nonprofit safety research lab to hundred-billion-dollar commercial giant. Achiam didn't specify his next move in his X post, but observers familiar with OpenAI's internal dynamics point to this as another case of the safety faction's ongoing exodus.

The Safety Camp's "Exodus"

Stretching out the timeline, Achiam's departure isn't an isolated incident.

In 2024, co-founder and chief scientist Ilya Sutskever left OpenAI to found Safe Superintelligence Inc. (SSI), taking a group of core researchers with him. In 2025, alignment team lead Jan Leike resigned for Anthropic, publicly criticizing OpenAI's "safety culture is dead." Since the start of 2026, several more senior safety and alignment researchers have left. Achiam's exit makes the talent drain thread all the clearer: OpenAI's "safety camp" is systematically eroding, with the "accelerationists" — those who believe rapid iteration and commercialization are the path to AGI — increasingly in control.

This talent composition shift will profoundly shape OpenAI's products and strategic direction. Simo's departure, while health-related, comes at a delicate juncture — announced the same day as GPT-5.6, during a critical IPO consideration window, and amid rumors of fractures in the Microsoft relationship. A number-two executive's exit inevitably creates a near-term product strategy vacuum. But OpenAI shipping GPT-5.6 on the same day also sends a signal: no matter who leaves, the product doesn't stop.

"When a company's safety team leaders leave one after another, it's hard not to wonder: are they the paranoid ones, or are we the naive ones?"— Anonymous AI safety researcher

For OpenAI, the current situation is best described as "products surging ahead, organizational undercurrents churning." GPT-5.6 is genuinely ahead technically, but leadership turmoil, talent drain, uncertainty around the Microsoft relationship, and IPO pressure — these organizational challenges won't disappear because a model shipped. On the dual tracks of AGI sprint and public listing, OpenAI needs to prove not just technical leadership but organizational stability and directional correctness.
