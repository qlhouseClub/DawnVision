朋友们，今天的AI芯片股市行情，已经不能用"刺激"来形容了，得用"魔幻现实主义"。

7月15日一开盘，韩国存储芯片龙头SK海力士直接闪崩，单日暴跌15%，市值一天蒸发掉将近300亿美元——这是什么概念？相当于一个中型独角兽公司一天就没了。而暴跌的原因呢？说出来你可能不信：SK海力士公布的二季度财报太好了，好到市场担心"这就是顶点"，然后就集体跑路了。

你品你细品：财报不及预期跌，财报超预期也跌。这股市，比女朋友的心思还难猜。

更魔幻的还在后面。同一天，国内存储芯片厂长鑫科技的招股书披露：部分员工的入股成本低至1毛钱/股。注意，是1毛钱，不是1块钱，是0.1元人民币。而按照目前的上市进度，一旦长鑫科技成功IPO，这些员工的浮盈可能达到几百倍甚至上千倍。

一边是韩国芯片巨头一天跌掉300亿美元，一边是中国芯片厂员工1毛钱入股即将财富自由。你说这世界魔幻不魔幻？

SK海力士为什么跌？好消息就是坏消息

先说SK海力士这波魔幻的暴跌。

SK海力士二季度业绩其实相当炸裂：营收17.8万亿韩元（约130亿美元），同比增长134%；净利润5.6万亿韩元（约41亿美元），同比增长超过10倍。HBM（高带宽内存，AI芯片必备）出货量环比增长50%，市场份额超过50%——这怎么看都是一份满分财报啊。

但股市的逻辑是：利好出尽就是利空。

投资者的逻辑是这样的：你业绩这么好，说明HBM供不应求的局面已经到了最紧张的时候；接下来呢？三星和海力士自己都在扩产，美光也在追，HBM供给上来了价格就会跌；而且AI大模型公司的资本开支增速已经开始放缓了，万一需求端不及预期呢？所以现在就是业绩顶点，赶紧跑。

你说这逻辑有没有道理？好像也有点道理。但一天跌15%，是不是反应过度了？资本市场的短期行为，永远比你想象的更夸张。这就像你在餐厅吃饭，菜刚上齐最好吃的时候，你突然想"这顿饭已经是最好吃的时候了，接下来只会越来越差"，然后直接起身买单走人，连甜品都不吃了。

1毛钱入股的财富神话，然后呢？

再看长鑫科技1毛钱入股这件事。

很多人看到"1毛钱入股"第一反应是"这是利益输送吧？"其实还真不是。这是科技公司创业过程中很常见的员工股权激励——公司早期估值低的时候给员工发期权，员工按当时的公允价值行权，等到公司上市了自然就赚翻了。你去看任何一个成功的科技公司IPO，早期员工的期权成本都是低得惊人的。

真正值得思考的是这件事的信号意义：国产存储芯片，真的要起来了。

长鑫科技是国内唯一一家能量产DDR4/DDR5内存的公司，跟长江存储（NAND闪存）一起被称为国产存储芯片的"双子星"。在中美科技博弈的大背景下，存储芯片国产替代是确定性最高的方向之一——国家大基金投了，地方政府投了，产业资本投了，现在IPO也快了。员工1毛钱入股的背后，是整个国家对国产存储芯片产业链的坚定投入。

当然了，股市永远是这样：在你最看好的时候给你泼一盆冷水（SK海力士暴跌），在你没注意的时候悄悄给你发糖（长鑫员工1毛入股）。AI芯片这个赛道，长期看好但短期波动巨大，普通人如果没有强大的心脏，真的不建议重仓炒股——你盯着那点收益，市场盯着你的本金。

今天就槽到这里，明天继续。

Friends, today's AI chip market action can't be described as "exciting" — it needs to be called "magical realism."

When markets opened July 15, Korean memory chip leader SK Hynix flash-crashed, plummeting 15% in a single session, wiping out nearly $30 billion in market cap in one day. For context: that's an entire mid-sized unicorn vanishing in hours. The reason for the crash? You won't believe it: SK Hynix's Q2 earnings were too good — so good the market worried "this is the peak" — and everyone ran for the exits at once.

Let that sink in: miss earnings and you drop, beat earnings and you also drop. The stock market is harder to read than your partner's mood.

It gets more surreal. Same day, the prospectus for Chinese memory chip maker Changxin Memory Technologies (CXMT) revealed that some employees received stock options at a strike price as low as 0.1 yuan per share. Not 1 yuan. 0.1 yuan. One mao. At the current IPO pace, if CXMT goes public successfully, those employees could see hundreds or even thousands of times returns on paper.

On one side: a Korean chip giant losing $30B in a day. On the other: Chinese chip factory employees getting in at 0.1 yuan on the verge of financial freedom. Is this world surreal or what?

Why SK Hynix Dropped: Good News Is Bad News

First, this absurd SK Hynix crash.

SK Hynix's Q2 results were actually explosive: revenue of 17.8 trillion won (~$13B), up 134% year-over-year; net profit of 5.6 trillion won (~$4.1B), up over 10x YoY. HBM (high-bandwidth memory, essential for AI chips) shipments up 50% quarter-over-quarter, market share over 50% — this looks like a perfect earnings report by any measure.

But stock market logic says: when all good news is priced in, good news becomes bad news.

Here's investor logic: your results are this good, meaning HBM supply-demand tightness has peaked. What's next? Samsung and SK Hynix are both ramping capacity, Micron is catching up, HBM supply goes up and prices come down. Plus, AI model companies' capex growth is already starting to slow — what if demand disappoints? So this must be the peak, sell now.

Is there some logic to that? Maybe. But a 15% single-day drop? That's overreaction, plain and simple. Short-term market behavior is always more extreme than you think. It's like sitting down at a restaurant, the food just arrived and it's the best bite of the meal, and you suddenly think "this is as good as it gets, it can only go downhill from here" — then you immediately pay the bill and leave, skipping dessert entirely.

The 1-Mao Option Wealth Myth — And What Comes After

Now, CXMT's 0.1 yuan options.

Many people see "0.1 yuan options" and immediately think "insider dealing?" Not really. This is standard employee equity incentive in tech startups: when valuations are low early on, companies issue options to employees at then-fair value, and by IPO those options are naturally worth a fortune. Look at any successful tech IPO — early employees' option strike prices are always shockingly low.

The real thing worth thinking about is the signal: domestic Chinese memory chips are actually getting somewhere.

CXMT is the only company in China mass-producing DDR4/DDR5 DRAM. Together with YMTC (NAND flash), they form the "twin stars" of Chinese domestic memory chips. Against the backdrop of US-China tech competition, domestic substitution in memory chips is one of the highest-certainty directions — national big funds invested, local governments invested, industrial capital invested, and now the IPO is approaching. Behind those 1-mao employee options is an entire nation's committed investment in the domestic memory chip supply chain.

Of course, the stock market is always like this: it throws cold water on you when you're most bullish (SK Hynix crashing 15%), and quietly hands you candy when you're not looking (CXMT employees getting in at 1 mao). The AI chip sector: great long-term thesis, massive short-term volatility. If you don't have a strong stomach, ordinary investors really shouldn't go heavy on individual stocks — you're watching for gains, the market's watching your principal.

That's all the roasting for today. See you tomorrow.
