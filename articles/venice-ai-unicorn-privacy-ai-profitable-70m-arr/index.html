Venice AI Unicorn
Privacy AI Hits Product-Market Fit

300万活跃用户、日均170万API调用、已盈利——这家主打"无审查、隐私优先"的AI平台用2年时间证明：用户愿意为不被监视的AI付费。

3 million active users, 1.7 million daily API calls, already profitable -- this "uncensored, privacy-first" AI platform used two years to prove: users will pay for AI that doesn't watch them.

No.007 2026.07.02 约 5 分钟阅读 ~5 min read

10亿美元。

这是主打"隐私优先、无审查"的AI平台Venice AI在最新一轮融资中的估值。A轮融资6500万美元，由加密风投Dragonfly领投，Coinbase Ventures等参投——更重要的是，这家成立仅两年的公司已经盈利，年化收入超过7000万美元。

隐私不是小众需求，是大众痛点

Venice AI的数据很能说明问题：网站独立访客85万，月活跃用户300万，日均API调用170万次。用户可以在平台上访问200多个AI模型（包括开源模型和通过路由访问OpenAI、Anthropic等闭源模型），所有用户输入在客户端加密、经外部代理路由后才处理，Venice自己的系统不存储任何用户数据。部分模型还提供端到端加密，不过需要付费订阅。

创始人Erik Voorhees的背景很有意思——他是早期比特币布道者，创立过比特币博彩网站Satoshi Dice和加密交易所ShapeShift，长期倡导用户隐私。他把Venice定位为"中立工具和中立平台"，和比特币的逻辑一脉相承："我认为，世界进入下一个阶段时每个人都被持续监视，这从安全角度来说其实危险得多——比某个人问了个有争议的问题危险得多。"

Venice的增长轨迹很说明问题。创始人承认刚上线时产品功能和ChatGPT差很远，用户用它纯粹是为了隐私；但现在功能已经和ChatGPT非常接近，当体验差距被弥合，"不被监视"就从一个小众极客需求变成了一个大众级的差异化卖点。

平台上的模型覆盖文本、图像、音频、视频生成，用户可以自由选择不同性能、质量和审查尺度的模型，甚至可以自定义AI角色。Venice还会修改开源模型的系统提示词，让它们回答得更开放，但不会额外添加限制。

AI商业化的第三条路

Venice AI的跑通，为AI商业化指出了第三条路。

过去一年，大家讨论最多的AI商业化路径要么是To C订阅（ChatGPT Plus、Claude Pro），要么是To B API/企业服务。Venice证明了"价值主张驱动的垂直平台"同样可以成立——你不需要做出比GPT更聪明的模型，你只需要在某一个价值维度（这里是隐私和自由）上做到极致，并且把体验做到足够接近主流产品，用户就愿意付费。

值得注意的是，虽然有加密货币背景（发了VVV和DIEM两个token，用户可以质押VVV铸造DIEM获得AI额度），但Voorhees透露只有约8%的用户用加密货币付费。这说明Venice的用户基本盘不是加密圈原住民，而是更广泛的、对隐私有需求的普通用户。

融资后Venice计划用这笔钱购买GPU、建设自己的数据中心，停止租赁GPU以提升毛利率。这和Base44等vibe coding平台自研模型的逻辑类似——当用户规模和调用量达到一定水平，自建基础设施的ROI就会转正。

当然，"无审查"也是一把双刃剑。AI精神健康风险、深度伪造、虚假信息等问题确实存在，完全不设限的平台很容易被滥用。Venice的回应和很多加密项目一样：工具是中立的，责任在使用者。这个立场在伦理层面争议很大，但从商业角度看，它确实切中了一个真实存在且规模不小的市场。

OpenAI同一天发布的ChatGPT adoption数据显示，ChatGPT在全球的使用量持续增长、覆盖更多语言和地区。但Venice的10亿估值和7000万ARR说明：不是所有用户都想要一个"政治正确"、被大公司监视、随时可能因为你问了敏感问题就拒绝回答的AI。AI市场不是赢家通吃，而是会分层——就像互联网既有Google也有Tor，既有Facebook也有Signal。

$1 billion.

That's the valuation of Venice AI -- the "privacy-first, uncensored" AI platform -- in its latest funding round. The $65 million Series A was led by crypto VC Dragonfly with participation from Coinbase Ventures and others -- more importantly, this barely two-year-old company is already profitable, with annualized revenue exceeding $70 million.

Privacy Isn't a Niche Need -- It's a Mass-Market Pain Point

Venice AI's numbers speak volumes: 850,000 unique website visitors, 3 million monthly active users, 1.7 million daily API calls. Users can access over 200 AI models on the platform (including open-source models and routed access to closed models like OpenAI and Anthropic). All user inputs are encrypted client-side and routed through external proxies before processing; Venice's own systems store zero user data. Some models offer end-to-end encryption, though that requires a paid subscription.

Founder Erik Voorhees's background is fascinating -- an early Bitcoin evangelist who founded the Bitcoin gambling site Satoshi Dice and crypto exchange ShapeShift, and a long-time advocate for user privacy. He positions Venice as a "neutral tool and neutral platform," consistent with Bitcoin's philosophy: "I think a world moving into its next phase where everyone is under constant surveillance is actually far more dangerous from a security perspective -- far more dangerous than someone asking a controversial question."

Venice's growth trajectory is telling. The founder admits that at launch the product's capabilities were far behind ChatGPT, and users used it purely for privacy; but now the functionality is very close to ChatGPT, and once the experience gap closes, "not being watched" transforms from a niche geek demand into a mass-market differentiator.

Models on the platform cover text, image, audio, and video generation. Users can freely choose models with different performance levels, quality, and censorship thresholds, and can even customize AI personas. Venice also modifies open-source models' system prompts to make them respond more openly, without adding extra restrictions.

The Third Path to AI Commercialization

Venice AI's success points to a third path for AI commercialization.

Over the past year, the most discussed AI monetization paths have been either consumer subscriptions (ChatGPT Plus, Claude Pro) or enterprise APIs/services. Venice proves that "value-proposition-driven vertical platforms" can work just as well -- you don't need to build a smarter model than GPT; you just need to go all-in on one value dimension (in this case, privacy and freedom) and get the experience close enough to mainstream products, and users will pay.

Notably, despite having crypto roots (it issued two tokens, VVV and DIEM; users can stake VVV to mint DIEM for AI credits), Voorhees revealed that only about 8% of users pay with cryptocurrency. This means Venice's core user base isn't crypto natives -- it's a broader audience of ordinary users who care about privacy.

Post-funding, Venice plans to use the capital to buy GPUs, build its own data centers, and stop leasing GPUs to improve gross margins. This follows the same logic as vibe coding platforms like Base44 building their own models -- when user scale and call volume reach a certain level, the ROI of owning your infrastructure turns positive.

Of course, "uncensored" is a double-edged sword. AI mental health risks, deepfakes, and misinformation are real problems, and completely unrestricted platforms can easily be abused. Venice's response, like many crypto projects: tools are neutral, responsibility lies with the user. That position is ethically controversial, but from a business standpoint, it clearly taps into a real and sizeable market.

OpenAI's ChatGPT adoption data, released the same day, shows ChatGPT usage continuing to grow globally across more languages and regions. But Venice's $1B valuation and $70M ARR demonstrate: not all users want a "politically correct" AI monitored by big corporations that might refuse to answer because you asked a sensitive question. The AI market isn't winner-takes-all -- it stratifies, just as the internet has both Google and Tor, both Facebook and Signal.

我们为自由和真正把用户当作成年人来尊重而优化——我认为这在今天很罕见。

—— Venice AI创始人 Erik Voorhees

We optimize for freedom and for truly treating users as adults -- something I think is rare today.

-- Erik Voorhees, Venice AI Founder
