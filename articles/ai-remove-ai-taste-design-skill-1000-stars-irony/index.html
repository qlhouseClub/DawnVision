一个设计Skill在GitHub上一日暴增1000星，火了。这个Skill是干什么的呢？它的目标很朴素——给Claude Code、Cursor和Codex用的，让它们生成的网页「不像AI生成的」。换句话说，它是一个「去AI味」的AI。

我第一次看到这个项目的时候，愣了三秒，然后笑出了声。AI生成的东西太有AI味了，以至于我们需要另一个AI来把AI味去掉——这是什么套娃行为？

「AI味」到底是个什么味

什么是「AI味」？做过设计的、写过代码的、看过AI生成内容的，大概都能心领神会，但很难精确描述。

在网页设计领域，「AI味」大概长这样：圆角永远是12px，阴影永远是那种「不深不浅刚刚好」的AI审美，配色永远是蓝紫色渐变，按钮永远是「Get Started」，hero section永远是「左边大标题右边示意图」，图标永远是来自同一套图标库的线性图标，间距永远是8px的整数倍……

不是说这样不好看，而是太「正确」了，正确到没有灵魂。就像一个长得完美无缺但你就是记不住的人——五官都在正确的位置，但没有任何特点。AI生成的设计就是这样：每一个元素都符合设计规范，但放在一起就是「一眼AI」。

更搞笑的是，这个「去AI味」的Skill本身也是AI写的。它的README里的示例代码是AI生成的，它的设计原则是从无数设计网站上学来的，它甚至可能是用另一个AI来测试效果的。整个链条就是：AI生成设计→AI发现有AI味→AI写了个去AI味的Skill→AI用这个Skill去除AI味→出来的结果……可能还是有AI味。

这就像什么呢？就像你雇了一个厨子，他做的菜永远是「料理包味」，于是你又雇了一个厨师专门负责「去除料理包味」，结果第二个厨子本身也是料理包培训出来的。你品，你细品。

这事儿荒诞在哪

这个「去AI味」Skill之所以好笑又好传，是因为它精准戳中了当前AI设计的一个集体痛点——所有人都在用AI做设计，所有人都觉得AI做出来的东西太像了，但所有人都还在继续用。

为什么？因为快啊。以前做一个落地页要找设计师花一周，现在用Cursor几分钟就生成了。虽然有AI味，但「有总比没有强」，「先上线再优化」，「等有预算了再请设计师改」——然后就没有然后了。

于是整个互联网的视觉风格正在以肉眼可见的速度趋同。以前每个网站都有自己的性格，有的酷、有的萌、有的极客、有的优雅。现在呢？你打开十个SaaS产品的官网，可能有八个是同一个AI生成的——配色差不多、排版差不多、配图风格差不多、甚至文案的语气都差不多。

最讽刺的是什么？最讽刺的是这个「去AI味」Skill本身也在GitHub上被疯狂fork，然后被集成到各种AI编程工具里。以后AI生成网页的时候会自动套用这个Skill，生成「去AI味」的设计——但当所有人都用同一个「去AI味」的模板，那「去AI味」本身不就变成了新的AI味吗？

"当AI味泛滥到一定程度，'去AI味'就会变成新的AI味。这是一个无限循环，没有尽头。"—— Cao编辑部

当然了，吐槽归吐槽，这个Skill能一天涨1000星，说明需求是真实的。大家确实烦透了千篇一律的AI设计，也确实需要一些方法让AI生成的东西更有个性、更有人味。

但我还是想提醒大家几句：

1. AI是工具，不是设计师。如果你自己都不知道好设计长什么样，AI也不会知道。想要独特的设计，先提高自己的审美。

2. 「去AI味」的最好方法是加入人的判断。而不是用另一个AI来替你判断。再好的Skill也只是一套规则，真正的设计感来自人的审美和经验。

3. 别太焦虑AI味。说实话，大部分产品死掉根本不是因为设计有AI味，而是因为产品本身就不行。产品跑通了，再请个好设计师改也不迟。

今天就槽到这里，明天继续。

Friends, today's AI circle punchline could easily serve as the foreword to "The Big Book of Absurd AI Moments 2026."

A design skill blew up on GitHub, gaining 1,000 stars in a single day. What does this skill do? Its goal is simple — for use with Claude Code, Cursor, and Codex, it makes the webpages they generate "not look like they were made by AI." In other words, it's an AI that "de-AI-ifies" things.

When I first saw this project, I froze for three seconds, then laughed out loud. AI-generated stuff has such a strong "AI taste" that we need another AI to remove the AI taste — what is this, Russian nesting dolls?

What Exactly Is 'AI Taste'?

What is "AI taste"? Anyone who's done design, written code, or seen AI-generated content probably knows it intuitively, but it's hard to describe precisely.

In web design, "AI taste" looks roughly like this: border-radius is always 12px, box shadows are always that "not too dark, not too light, just right" AI aesthetic, color schemes are always blue-purple gradients, buttons always say "Get Started," hero sections are always "big headline on the left, illustration on the right," icons are always line icons from the same icon library, spacing is always multiples of 8px…

It's not that it looks bad. It's that it's too "correct" — correct to the point of having no soul. Like a person with perfectly proportioned features whom you can never remember — everything is in the right place, but there's nothing distinctive about them. AI-generated design is like that: every element follows design specs, but put together it's just "instantly recognizable as AI."

The funnier part is, this "remove AI taste" skill itself was written by AI. The example code in its README is AI-generated. Its design principles are learned from countless design websites. It might even be tested using another AI. The whole chain is: AI generates design → AI detects AI taste → AI writes a skill to remove AI taste → AI uses the skill to remove AI taste → and the result… probably still tastes like AI.

Here's the analogy: you hire a cook, and everything he makes tastes like "meal kit flavor." So you hire another chef specifically to "remove the meal kit flavor" — except the second chef was also trained on meal kits. Think about it. Really think about it.

What's So Absurd About This

The reason this "remove AI taste" skill is both funny and viral is that it precisely hits a collective pain point in current AI design: everyone's using AI for design, everyone thinks AI-made stuff all looks the same, and everyone keeps using it anyway.

Why? Because it's fast. It used to take a designer a week to make a landing page. Now you generate one with Cursor in minutes. Sure it has AI taste, but "something is better than nothing," "ship first, optimize later," "we'll hire a designer to fix it when we have budget" — and then there is no later.

So the visual style of the entire internet is converging at a pace visible to the naked eye. It used to be that every website had its own personality — some were cool, some cute, some geeky, some elegant. Now? Open ten SaaS product websites, and eight might have been generated by the same AI — similar colors, similar layouts, similar illustration styles, even similar tones in the copy.

The most ironic part? This very "remove AI taste" skill is being forked like crazy on GitHub and integrated into all kinds of AI coding tools. Soon AI will automatically apply this skill when generating webpages, producing "de-AI-ified" designs — but when everyone uses the same "de-AI-ification" template, doesn't "de-AI-ification" itself become the new AI taste?

"When AI taste becomes ubiquitous enough, 'removing AI taste' becomes the new AI taste. It's an infinite loop with no exit."— The Cao Editorial Desk

Of course, jokes aside, the fact that this skill gained 1,000 stars in a day means the demand is real. People are genuinely sick of cookie-cutter AI design, and they genuinely need ways to make AI output more distinctive and more human.

But I still want to remind everyone of a few things:

1. AI is a tool, not a designer. If you don't know what good design looks like yourself, AI won't know either. Want unique design? Improve your own taste first.

2. The best way to 'remove AI taste' is to add human judgment. Not to use another AI to do the judging for you. Even the best skill is just a set of rules. Real design sense comes from human aesthetics and experience.

3. Don't stress too much about AI taste. Honestly, most products die not because their design tastes like AI, but because the product itself sucks. Once your product works, hiring a good designer to fix the design is never too late.
