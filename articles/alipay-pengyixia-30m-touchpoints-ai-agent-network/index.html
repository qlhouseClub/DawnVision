3000万台。这是支付宝"碰一下"设备已经铺设到线下的规模——餐馆的桌角、便利店的收银台、自动售货机的屏幕前，这个小小的NFC触点已经覆盖了中国绝大多数城市的消费场景。

7月8日，支付宝宣布完成一次关键升级：百万级商家侧碰一下设备整体升级为"碰设备Agent"，同步推出面向中小商家的"碰万物Agent"服务平台，以及面向开发者的"AI开放底座"。这不是一次普通的产品更新，而是线下支付入口正式向AI Agent网络转型的宣言。

从支付工具到经营Agent

理解这次升级的意义，要回到"碰一下"的产品本质。

碰一下最初是支付宝对标微信扫码支付推出的NFC近场支付产品——手机碰一下设备就能完成付款，比扫二维码更快。但在AI时代，这个硬件触点的价值被重新发现了：它不只是一个支付入口，还是一个离消费者和商家场景最近的AI交互节点。

升级为"碰设备Agent"意味着什么？以前碰一下只能做一件事——付款。现在碰一下设备背后有一个AI Agent，它可以理解商家的经营需求和消费者的场景需求，主动完成一系列连锁动作：消费者碰一下点单，Agent不仅完成支付，还能根据历史消费记录推荐加购、自动核销优惠券、积分入账、甚至预约下次到店；商家碰一下设备，Agent能回答经营问题、生成当日经营报表、建议补货清单、自动回复顾客评价。

"碰万物Agent"平台则把这个能力开放给了中小商家。不需要技术团队，不需要写代码，商家在支付宝商家后台用自然语言描述自己的生意，Agent就能自动生成适合自己店铺的AI服务逻辑——奶茶店的碰一下可以推荐新品，理发店的碰一下可以预约发型师，健身房的碰一下可以推荐私教课程。

线下入口的AI战争进入第二阶段

微信和支付宝的Agent战争，从线上App打到了线下硬件。

过去几个月，微信侧的动作是"小微"灰度测试、企业微信推大圆Agent、企业微信和出门问问合作发布首款AI录音硬件TicNote。微信的策略是从通讯和工作场景切入，把AI能力嵌入聊天框和工作流。支付宝的策略则更直接：从支付和经营场景切入，把已经铺到千万级别的线下硬件触点直接升级为Agent节点。

这两条路径各有优势。微信掌握着12亿月活的聊天入口，用户每天打开微信几十次；支付宝掌握着千万级的线下支付触点和完整的商家经营数据，消费者和商家的交易链路最完整。如果说第一阶段是线上App里的Agent入口争夺，第二阶段就是线下物理世界的Agent触点争夺——谁能把AI能力嵌入到消费者每天都会碰的硬件节点里，谁就能拿到AI时代线下商业的入场券。

对开发者来说，这次升级最值得关注的是"AI开放底座"——第三方开发者可以基于碰一下的硬件触点开发自己的Agent服务，就像当年小程序开放生态一样。这意味着碰一下不只是支付宝自己的产品，而是一个可以接入各种AI服务的硬件平台。

从扫码支付到碰一下支付，用了将近十年；从碰一下支付到碰一下Agent，只用了不到一年。线下商业的AI化速度，比所有人预想的都要快。当你下次吃饭碰一下付款时，要知道和你交互的不再是一个冰冷的扣款系统，而是一个懂这家店生意的AI Agent。

明天见。

Thirty million devices. That's how many Alipay "Peng Yi Xia" (Tap) devices have been deployed offline — on restaurant tables, convenience store checkout counters, vending machine screens — this little NFC touchpoint already covers consumption scenarios in the vast majority of Chinese cities.

On July 8, Alipay announced a pivotal upgrade: millions of merchant-side Tap devices are being upgraded to "Tap Device Agents," alongside the launch of a "Tap Everything Agent" service platform for SMBs and an "AI Open Foundation" for developers. This isn't a routine product update — it's a declaration that offline payment entry points are officially transforming into an AI Agent network.

From Payment Tool to Business Agent

To understand the significance of this upgrade, return to the essential nature of the Tap product.

Tap originally launched as Alipay's NFC proximity payment answer to WeChat's QR code payments — tap your phone to the device to pay, faster than scanning a QR code. But in the AI era, this hardware touchpoint's value has been rediscovered: it's not just a payment entry point; it's the AI interaction node closest to both consumer and merchant scenarios.

What does upgrading to a "Tap Device Agent" mean? Previously, Tap could only do one thing — process payments. Now, behind each Tap device sits an AI Agent that can understand merchant operational needs and consumer contextual needs, proactively completing a chain of actions: a consumer taps to order, and the Agent not only processes payment but also recommends add-ons based on purchase history, automatically redeems coupons, credits loyalty points, even books the next visit. When a merchant taps the device, the Agent answers business questions, generates daily operations reports, suggests restocking lists, and automatically responds to customer reviews.

The "Tap Everything Agent" platform opens this capability to SMBs. No tech team needed, no code required — merchants describe their business in natural language in the Alipay merchant backend, and the Agent automatically generates AI service logic tailored to their shop: a bubble tea shop's Tap can recommend new arrivals; a hair salon's Tap can book stylists; a gym's Tap can recommend personal training sessions.

The Offline Entry AI War Enters Phase Two

The Agent war between WeChat and Alipay has moved from online apps to offline hardware.

In recent months, WeChat's moves were "Xiao Wei" gray-testing, Enterprise WeChat launching the Da Yuan Agent, and Enterprise WeChat partnering with Mobvoi to release its first AI recording hardware, the TicNote. WeChat's strategy is entering through communication and work scenarios, embedding AI into chat boxes and workflows. Alipay's strategy is more direct: entering through payment and business scenarios, directly upgrading tens of millions of already-deployed offline hardware touchpoints into Agent nodes.

These two paths each have advantages. WeChat holds a 1.2 billion MAU chat entry point where users open the app dozens of times daily; Alipay holds tens of millions of offline payment touchpoints and complete merchant business data, with the most complete transaction chain between consumers and merchants. If phase one was the Agent entry point battle inside online apps, phase two is the Agent touchpoint battle in the physical offline world — whoever can embed AI capabilities into hardware nodes that consumers touch every day wins the ticket to AI-era offline commerce.

For developers, the most notable part of this upgrade is the "AI Open Foundation" — third-party developers can build their own Agent services on top of Tap hardware touchpoints, much like the Mini Program open ecosystem back in the day. This means Tap isn't just Alipay's own product; it's a hardware platform that can plug into all kinds of AI services.

From QR code payments to Tap payments took nearly a decade. From Tap payments to Tap Agents took less than a year. The AI-ification of offline commerce is moving faster than anyone expected. Next time you tap to pay at a restaurant, know that what you're interacting with is no longer a cold billing system — it's an AI Agent that understands this shop's business.
