NotebookLM One-Click TikTok
AI Content Production Goes Vertical

Google NotebookLM新增60秒竖屏AI视频生成，基于用户上传资料自动制作TikTok风格内容。同一周图书馆App Libby宣布过滤AI内容——创作和过滤同时加速。

Google NotebookLM adds 60-second vertical AI video generation, automatically producing TikTok-style content from user-uploaded materials. The same week, library app Libby announced AI content filtering — creation and filtering accelerate simultaneously.

No.008 2026.07.03 约 5 分钟阅读 ~5 min read

以后做"3分钟看懂一篇论文"的短视频，可能只需要点一下按钮。

7月1日，Google宣布NotebookLM新增AI Clips功能：可以把你上传到NotebookLM的任何资料——论文、笔记、PDF、研究报告——自动生成一段60秒的竖屏TikTok/Reels/Shorts风格短视频。视频包含自动配音、字幕、视觉素材和关键信息摘要，直接适配短视频平台的竖屏格式。AI内容创作工具，终于杀到了短视频这个最大的内容战场。

从音频播客到竖屏视频，NotebookLM的进化路径

NotebookLM最初是一个AI笔记助手，后来凭借"AI播客"功能出圈——两个人声对话风格的音频摘要，把枯燥的论文变成了像电台节目一样好懂的播客。这次的AI Clips是同一个逻辑的延伸：把复杂信息压缩成适合移动端消费的短格式内容。

Google给出的demo展示了一个典型场景：上传一份关于气候变化的研究报告，NotebookLM自动生成一段60秒竖屏视频，有AI配音讲解核心发现，有配合内容的图片和文字动画，有字幕，有片头片尾——基本就是一个可以直接发TikTok的成品。这个功能面向Google AI Ultra和Pro订阅用户开放。

这对内容创作者意味着什么？意味着"把长内容变成短视频"这个最耗时的环节，可能被AI大幅压缩。过去一个博主把一篇深度文章做成3条短视频，可能需要半天时间写脚本、找素材、剪辑；现在NotebookLM可以几分钟内生成初稿，创作者只需要调整和优化。一个人运营多个账号的"矩阵玩法"门槛进一步降低。

创作和过滤同时加速

有意思的是，就在NotebookLM让AI生成内容变得更容易的同一周，图书馆借阅App Libby（OverDrive旗下）宣布将推出AI内容过滤功能。OverDrive新任CEO Marc DeBevoise表示："AI是我们的新前沿"，但平台会让读者能够筛选出哪些书是AI生成的。Tidal也宣布从7月15日起不给100% AI生成的音乐支付版税，但会打上标签而不是 outright 禁止。

AI内容的"创作"和"标识/过滤"正在同时加速，这形成了一个有趣的张力：生产端越来越容易，消费端越来越需要辨别真伪。平台必须在"拥抱AI提高效率"和"保护人类创作者权益"之间找到平衡。Tidal选择不禁止但标签化+demonetize，Libby选择给读者选择权——这些都是务实的中间路线。

Meta也悄悄上线了一个有趣的产品：Pocket，一个用vibe coding（AI辅助编程）开发的实验性游戏应用，用户可以用文字提示生成和分享互动小游戏。这是Meta在AI创作工具上的又一个尝试，虽然还很早期，但说明大厂都在赌"AI降低创作门槛"这个方向。

Gemini Spark也在本周登陆Mac平台，带来实时追踪和更多应用支持。Google正在把它的AI助手从网页端推向桌面端，和Apple Intelligence、Copilot正面竞争。

AI内容创作工具正在从"生成文字"进化到"生成完整的多模态内容包"——文字、图片、音频、视频，一条龙服务。对内容创作者来说，这是最好的时代也是最坏的时代：生产工具前所未有地强大，但竞争也前所未有地激烈，差异化的门槛从"能不能做出来"变成了"有没有独特的观点和审美"。

In the future, making a “3-minute explainer on a paper” short video might just take one click.

On July 1, Google announced a new AI Clips feature for NotebookLM: any material you upload to NotebookLM — papers, notes, PDFs, research reports — can be automatically turned into a 60-second vertical TikTok/Reels/Shorts-style video. Videos include AI voiceover, subtitles, visual assets, and key information summaries, directly formatted for short video platforms. AI content creation tools have finally arrived at short-form video, the largest content battlefield.

From Audio Podcasts to Vertical Video: NotebookLM's Evolution Path

NotebookLM started as an AI note-taking assistant, then broke out with its “AI Podcast” feature — two-voice conversational audio summaries that turned dry papers into radio-show-like digestible podcasts. AI Clips extends the same logic: compressing complex information into short-form content suitable for mobile consumption.

Google's demo shows a typical scenario: upload a research report on climate change, and NotebookLM auto-generates a 60-second vertical video with AI voiceover explaining key findings, images and text animations synced to content, subtitles, intro and outro — basically a finished product ready for TikTok. The feature is available to Google AI Ultra and Pro subscribers.

What does this mean for content creators? It means the most time-consuming step — turning long-form content into short videos — could be dramatically compressed by AI. Previously a creator taking a deep article and making three short videos might spend half a day scripting, sourcing, and editing; now NotebookLM can generate a first draft in minutes, leaving creators only to adjust and refine. The barrier to running multiple accounts in a ‘matrix playbook’ drops further.

Creation and Filtering Accelerate Simultaneously

Interestingly, the same week NotebookLM made AI content generation easier, library borrowing app Libby (by OverDrive) announced it would launch AI content filtering. OverDrive's new CEO Marc DeBevoise said: “AI is our new frontier,” but the platform will let readers filter out which books are AI-generated. Tidal also announced that starting July 15 it won't pay royalties for 100% AI-generated music, but will label it rather than outright banning it.

AI content “creation” and “labeling/filtering” are accelerating simultaneously, creating an interesting tension: production gets ever easier, while consumption requires ever more discernment of what's real. Platforms must balance “embracing AI for efficiency” and “protecting human creators' rights.” Tidal chose not to ban but to label and demonetize; Libby chose to give readers choice — both pragmatic middle paths.

Meta also quietly launched an interesting product: Pocket, an experimental gaming app built with vibe coding (AI-assisted programming), where users can generate and share interactive mini-games via text prompts. It's another Meta attempt at AI creation tools; still early, but it indicates Big Tech is betting on “AI lowering creation barriers.”

Gemini Spark also landed on Mac this week, bringing real-time tracking and more app support. Google is pushing its AI assistant from web to desktop, going head-to-head with Apple Intelligence and Copilot.

AI content creation tools are evolving from “generating text” to “generating complete multimodal content packages” — text, images, audio, video, full-service. For content creators, it's the best of times and the worst of times: production tools are unprecedentedly powerful, but competition is unprecedentedly fierce; the differentiation bar shifts from ‘can you make it’ to ‘do you have unique perspective and taste’.

当人人都能一键生成短视频时，唯一不可替代的是你想说什么——而不是你怎么把它做出来。
