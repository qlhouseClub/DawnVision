7月10日，《每日经济新闻》独家获悉，月之暗面（Moonshot AI）旗下的"Kimi信用卡"合作方已经敲定美国运通（American Express）和中国农业银行，将于当日正式对外发行。这是全球首张AI原生信用卡，也是中国大模型公司第一次深度切入金融支付基础设施。

AI原生卡到底是什么？

先搞清楚一个问题：Kimi信用卡和银行经常发的"联名信用卡"有什么区别？

传统的联名信用卡本质上还是银行的卡——品牌方出个名字和logo，银行负责审批、风控、清算、客服，用户得到的是一些品牌相关的消费权益（折扣、积分、返现）。你钱包里那些航空公司联名卡、电商平台联名卡，都是这个逻辑。

Kimi信用卡的定位是"AI原生"，这意味着AI不是贴在卡面上的营销标签，而是渗透在信用卡核心体验里的智能层。目前披露的信息还比较有限，但"AI原生"至少包含几个层面：第一，智能消费分析——基于Kimi的自然语言理解能力，用户可以用对话方式查询消费记录、分析支出结构、获得个性化理财建议；第二，智能风控和反欺诈——大模型实时分析交易模式，识别异常消费；第三，也是最具想象力的方向——智能体支付。

报道中明确提到，Kimi信用卡"未来将探索智能体支付"。这四个字的分量很重。智能体支付意味着什么？意味着你的AI Agent可以在授权范围内自主完成消费决策和支付动作——自动比价下单、自动续订服务、自动调整消费计划，不需要你每次手动确认。当AI Agent从"帮你查信息"进化到"帮你花钱"，支付的入口和体验会被彻底重构。

为什么是美国运通+农行？

Kimi信用卡选择的合作方组合非常耐人寻味。

美国运通（Amex）是全球最大的银行卡清算组织之一，和Visa、Mastercard并列，但走的是高端商旅差异化路线。和美国运通合作，意味着Kimi信用卡可能不止是一张国内卡——通过Amex的全球清算网络，这张卡具备了国际化支付能力。对于一家有全球化野心的AI公司来说，这一步棋的远见不容忽视。

中国农业银行是国内四大行之一，拥有庞大的个人客户基础和完善的发卡、风控、客服体系。和农行合作解决了最关键的问题：银行牌照和发卡资质。信用卡业务是强监管领域，没有银行牌照寸步难行——农行作为发卡行，Kimi作为技术和体验提供方，这种"银行+科技公司"的合作模式是当前监管框架下最可行的路径。

值得注意的是，AI公司切入金融基础设施不是第一次——支付宝最初就是淘宝的担保交易工具，后来演变成中国最大的第三方支付平台；微信支付从红包切入，成为国民级支付工具。但Kimi信用卡的不同之处在于：它是从AI入口反向切入支付，而不是从电商或社交场景延伸出来。Kimi目前拥有数千万月活用户，如果这些用户的消费场景能通过信用卡被Kimi锁定，那它就从一个"聊天工具"变成了一个覆盖消费决策、支付执行、金融服务的超级入口。

大模型公司的商业化路径一直是行业难题——按token收费赚的是辛苦钱，企业定制化服务天花板可见。支付和金融可能是那条最肥美的赛道：交易佣金、信贷利息、理财代销，每一项都是真金白银的收入。Kimi这一步，可能为整个大模型行业的商业化探索出一条新路。

明天见。

An AI large-model company is launching a credit card. Not a co-branded marketing card — a genuine AI-native credit card, with a payment network, an issuing bank, and payment license backing.

On July 10, National Business Daily exclusively reported that Moonshot AI's "Kimi Credit Card" has confirmed American Express and Agricultural Bank of China as partners, with an official launch the same day. This is the world's first AI-native credit card and the first time a Chinese LLM company has made a deep foray into financial payment infrastructure.

What Does "AI-Native Card" Actually Mean?

First, a key question: how is the Kimi Credit Card different from the "co-branded credit cards" banks routinely issue?

Traditional co-branded cards are fundamentally the bank's card — the brand provides a name and logo, the bank handles approval, risk control, clearing, and customer service, and users get brand-related perks (discounts, points, cashback). The airline cards and e-commerce cards in your wallet all work this way.

The Kimi Credit Card is positioned as "AI-native," meaning AI isn't a marketing sticker on the card face — it's an intelligence layer woven into core credit card experiences. Disclosed details are still limited, but "AI-native" implies at least three layers: first, intelligent spending analysis — leveraging Kimi's natural language understanding, users can query transactions, analyze spending patterns, and get personalized financial advice via conversation; second, intelligent risk control and anti-fraud — real-time LLM analysis of transaction patterns to flag anomalies; third, and most provocative — agent payments.

The report explicitly states the Kimi Credit Card will "explore agent payments" going forward. Those four words carry enormous weight. Agent payments mean your AI agent could, within authorized bounds, autonomously make spending decisions and execute payments — auto-compare and place orders, auto-renew services, auto-adjust spending plans, without manual confirmation each time. When AI agents evolve from "helping you look up information" to "helping you spend money," payment entry points and experiences get completely rearchitected.

Why American Express + Agricultural Bank of China?

The partner combination Kimi chose is remarkably deliberate.

American Express (Amex) is one of the world's largest card payment networks, alongside Visa and Mastercard, with a premium travel and lifestyle differentiation. Partnering with Amex means the Kimi Credit Card likely isn't just a domestic card — through Amex's global clearing network, it has international payment capability. For an AI company with global ambitions, this is a forward-looking chess move.

Agricultural Bank of China (ABC) is one of China's Big Four state-owned banks, with a massive retail customer base and mature card issuance, risk control, and customer service systems. Partnering with ABC solves the critical problem: banking license and card issuance qualification. Credit cards are a heavily regulated sector — you can't operate without a bank license. ABC as issuer and Kimi as technology and experience provider is the most viable model under the current regulatory framework.

Notably, this isn't the first time an AI company has entered financial infrastructure — Alipay started as Taobao's escrow tool and evolved into China's largest third-party payment platform; WeChat Pay entered through red envelopes and became a national payment utility. But Kimi Credit Card differs in one crucial way: it's entering payments in reverse from an AI entry point, rather than extending from e-commerce or social scenarios. Kimi currently has tens of millions of MAUs. If those users' spending scenarios can be locked in through a credit card, Kimi transforms from a "chat tool" into a super gateway covering consumption decisions, payment execution, and financial services.

LLM companies have long struggled with commercialization — per-token pricing is hard labor, and enterprise custom services have visible ceilings. Payments and finance may be the fattest track: transaction fees, credit interest, wealth management distribution — each generating real-money revenue. Kimi's move may blaze a new commercialization trail for the entire LLM industry.
