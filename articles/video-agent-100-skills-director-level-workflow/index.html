AI视频的三个阶段：从玩具到工具到工作台

AI视频发展到今天，经历了三个明显的阶段。

第一阶段：生成玩具（2023-2024）。代表产品是早期的Sora演示、Runway Gen-2、Pika。这个阶段的特点是"效果惊艳但没用"——你输入一段文字，它生成几秒钟的视频，看起来很酷，但时长太短、一致性太差、可控性太弱，生成的素材基本没法直接用到成片里。主要用途是做社交媒体上的猎奇短视频。

第二阶段：素材工具（2025-2026初）。代表产品是可灵AI、即梦、Sora正式版。这个阶段的特点是"可用但割裂"——你可以生成几分钟的视频了，人物一致性和物理真实感都不错，但AI生成只是整个视频制作流程中的一个环节。你还是需要把素材下载下来，导入Premiere或剪映，手动剪辑、调色、配乐、加字幕。AI帮你做了"原料"，但"炒菜"还是得自己来。

第三阶段：工作流平台（2026年中开始）。就是这次发布的视频Agent代表的方向——AI不再只是"生成素材的工具"，而是接管整个创作工作流。从脚本到分镜、从素材生成到剪辑、从调色到配乐到字幕，AI Agent全程参与，你是导演，它们是你的摄制团队。

为什么工作流比生成能力更重要？

一个3分钟的短视频，背后是几十个决策点：开头怎么吸引注意力？哪里需要停顿？哪里加转场？节奏怎么控制？BGM从哪里进、哪里出？字幕字体用什么？花字怎么加才不突兀？调色风格怎么统一？这些决策加在一起，才构成了最终的视频质量。

以前的AI视频工具解决的是"有没有素材"的问题，现在的视频Agent解决的是"怎么把素材变成好内容"的问题。这是质的区别。

Three Stages of AI Video: Toy → Tool → Workbench

AI video has evolved through three distinct phases to reach today.

Phase 1: Generation toys (2023-2024). Represented by early Sora demos, Runway Gen-2, Pika. The defining trait was "impressive but useless" — input text, get a few seconds of video, looks cool, but too short, too inconsistent, too uncontrollable to use in finished productions. Mostly used for novelty short-form social content.

Phase 2: Clip tools (2025-early 2026). Represented by Kling, Jimeng, and the official Sora release. Traits: "usable but siloed" — you could generate minutes of video, character consistency and physics improved, but AI generation was just one step in the production pipeline. You still downloaded clips, imported into Premiere or CapCut, manually edited, color graded, scored, subtitled. AI made the "ingredients" but you still had to "cook."

Phase 3: Workflow platforms (starting mid-2026). This is what the new video agent represents — AI is no longer just a "clip generation tool." It's taking over the entire creative workflow. From script to storyboard, clip generation to editing, color grading to scoring to subtitles, AI agents participate throughout. You're the director; they're your film crew.

Why Workflow Matters More Than Generation Quality

Many people still compare "who generates sharper video" or "who has better consistency," but professional creators know video production is never just about "generating a clip."

A three-minute short video involves dozens of decisions: how does the opening hook attention? Where do you pause? Where do transitions go? How do you control pacing? Where does BGM enter and exit? What subtitle font? How do you add text overlays without being jarring? How do you unify the color grade? These decisions together make the final video quality.

Previous AI video tools solved "do we have clips" — now video agents solve "how do we turn clips into good content". That's a qualitative leap.
