10万字的会议速记，什么概念？按正常语速，大约是10-12小时的会议录音转写结果。正常人要把这堆素材整理成一份清晰的会议纪要，快的话要一整天，慢的话要两三天。

金山办公最新发布的WPS AI Agent说：给我，几分钟。

7月24日，量子位报道金山办公上线了新一代AI Agent能力——不再是以前那种"帮你写个邮件开头""给你列个大纲"的Copilot模式，而是直接处理长文档素材，输出可以直接交付的成品。10万字会议速记丢进去，几分钟后出来的是格式规范、逻辑清晰、有摘要有决议有行动项的会议纪要，甚至可以直接生成PPT版本给老板汇报。

从"辅助"到"交付"：办公AI的范式跃迁

过去两年，所有办公软件都在加AI功能，但大部分都是"Copilot模式"——AI给你打个下手，帮你补全句子、润色文字、翻译一下，最终决策和整合还是要你自己来。你让Word帮你写报告，它给你写个开头，后面还是得你自己写完。

WPS这次的新Agent，标志着办公AI进入了一个新阶段：从"辅助你写"变成"替你写完"。

这背后有几个关键能力的突破。第一是超长上下文处理——10万字相当于一本长篇小说的篇幅，能稳定处理这种长度的输入而不丢失关键信息，对模型的长上下文能力和信息提取能力是很大的考验。第二是结构化输出能力——不是给你一堆文字，而是按照会议纪要的标准格式输出，有明确的章节、重点标注、待办事项。第三是多格式交付——同一份内容可以输出为Word文档、PPT演示文稿、甚至思维导图。

量子位的报道里有一句话很到位："重做一遍WPS"。这不是在现有WPS里加一个AI按钮，而是从底层用Agent思维重构办公软件的工作流。以前的工作流是：人收集素材→人整理素材→人撰写草稿→人排版→人输出。Agent时代的工作流是：人丢素材→Agent处理→人审核→Agent输出。人的角色从"制作者"变成了"审核者"。

国产办公软件的Agent竞赛

金山办公不是唯——个在做办公Agent的，但它可能是目前在中文场景下做得最深入的。

微软Copilot当然很强，但它在中文长文档处理、中文会议场景理解、国内办公格式兼容性上始终有一层隔阂。飞书的AI能力也在快速迭代，但飞书更多是在协作平台层面做Agent，而不是在文档生产工具层面。WPS作为国内市场份额最大的办公软件，在中文文档处理上有二十多年的积累，现在把这些积累和大模型Agent能力结合，爆发力是很强的。

"以前评估AI写作工具看它'写得好不好'，以后要看它'能不能直接交活'。"—— 一位企业数字化负责人

这对内容创作者、行政人员、咨询顾问、分析师等"靠文档吃饭"的职业影响是巨大的。以前整理会议纪要、写行业报告、做竞品分析，可能要花60%的时间在"整理素材和搭框架"这种机械劳动上，真正的思考只占40%。Agent把那60%的机械劳动接过去之后，人的时间可以更多花在判断、思考、创意这些AI做不好的事情上。

当然，问题也存在。AI整理的会议纪要会不会漏掉关键信息？会不会错误总结某人的观点？生成的PPT是不是像那么回事？这些都需要时间验证。但方向是明确的：办公软件的AI能力，正在从"锦上添花的小功能"变成"不可或缺的生产力工具"。

当10万字速记几分钟就能变成可交付的成品时，你再花一整天去整理会议纪要，就不是"认真负责"，而是"不会用工具"了。

明天见。

What does 100,000 words of meeting transcripts look like? At normal speaking pace, it's about 10–12 hours of recorded meeting time. For a normal person to turn that raw material into a clean set of meeting minutes — a full day if you're fast, two or three days if you're not.

Kingsoft WPS's newly released AI Agent says: hand it over, and you'll have it in minutes.

On July 24, QbitAI reported that Kingsoft Office had launched a new generation of AI Agent capabilities for WPS — no longer the old Copilot mode of "help you write an email opener" or "generate an outline," but the ability to process long document materials directly and output deliverable finished products. Feed it 100,000 words of raw meeting transcripts, and in minutes you get properly formatted, logically structured meeting minutes with summaries, resolutions, and action items — even a ready-to-present PowerPoint version for your boss.

From "Assisting" to "Delivering": A Paradigm Shift in Office AI

For the past two years, every office app has been adding AI features — but most have stayed in "Copilot mode": AI plays assistant, helping you autocomplete sentences, polish text, or translate, with final decisions and integration still on you. Ask Word to write a report, and it writes an intro — the rest is still on you.

WPS's new Agent marks a new phase in office AI: from "helping you write" to "writing it for you."

Several capability breakthroughs underpin this. First, long-context processing — 100,000 words is roughly novel-length, and reliably processing inputs at that scale without losing key information is a serious test of a model's long-context and information extraction capabilities. Second, structured output — not just a wall of text, but output formatted to meeting-minutes standards with clear sections, key-point highlights, and to-do items. Third, multi-format delivery — the same content can be output as a Word document, a PowerPoint deck, or even a mind map.

A line in the QbitAI piece hits the mark: "Rebuilding WPS from scratch." This isn't adding an AI button to existing WPS — it's reconstructing office workflows from the ground up with agentic thinking. The old workflow: human collects materials → human organizes materials → human drafts → human formats → human outputs. The agent-era workflow: human dumps materials → Agent processes → human reviews → Agent outputs. The human shifts from "maker" to "reviewer."

The Agent Race in Chinese Office Software

Kingsoft isn't the only company building office agents, but it may be the deepest in Chinese-language scenarios today.

Microsoft Copilot is certainly powerful, but it always hits a wall in Chinese long-document processing, Chinese meeting scenario understanding, and domestic office format compatibility. Feishu's AI capabilities are also iterating rapidly, but Feishu is building agents at the collaboration-platform layer rather than the document-production layer. WPS, as the office suite with the largest market share in China, has over two decades of accumulated expertise in Chinese document processing — combining that heritage with LLM agent capabilities creates real leverage.

"We used to evaluate AI writing tools on 'how well does it write'; from now on we evaluate on 'can it deliver the finished product.'"— An enterprise digitalization lead

The impact on content creators, administrative staff, consultants, analysts, and other "document workers" is enormous. Organizing meeting minutes, writing industry reports, doing competitive analysis used to mean 60% of your time spent on the mechanical work of "organizing materials and building frameworks," with only 40% on actual thinking. Agents taking over that 60% of mechanical labor frees up human time for judgment, thinking, and creativity — the things AI can't do well.

Questions remain, of course. Will AI-generated minutes miss key information? Will it mis-summarize someone's point? Will the auto-generated slides actually look presentable? Time will tell. But the direction is clear: office AI is evolving from a "nice-to-have feature" to an "indispensable productivity tool."

When 100,000 words of transcripts can become a deliverable in minutes, spending a full day organizing meeting minutes stops being "diligent" and starts being "not knowing how to use the tools."
