ChatGPT Work Enterprise Agent Launches
OpenAI Moves from Chat to Work

能像人类员工一样持续工作、处理多步骤任务、长时间运行——ChatGPT Work标志着OpenAI的战略转向：从"回答问题的工具"变成"完成工作的同事"。

Works continuously like a human employee, handles multi-step tasks, runs for extended periods — ChatGPT Work marks OpenAI's strategic pivot: from "a tool that answers questions" to "a colleague that gets work done."

No.013 2026.07.14 约 5 分钟阅读 ~5 min read

7月10日，OpenAI一口气发布了两件大事：GPT-5.6系列模型，以及ChatGPT Work。

大多数人的注意力都被GPT-5.6吸引过去了——Sol/Terra/Luna三档齐发、Ultra多Agent模式、能力又提升了多少多少。但在我看来，真正重要的反而是ChatGPT Work这个新产品。

因为它标志着OpenAI的一次战略转向：从"对话AI"走向"工作AI"。以前的ChatGPT是你问它答、一问一答；现在的ChatGPT Work是你给它任务，它自己规划、自己执行、自己搞定，像一个真正的员工一样持续工作。

这不是程度上的差别，这是范式上的变化。

ChatGPT Work到底是什么

官方定义是"企业级智能体"，但这个说法太抽象了。用大白话讲：ChatGPT Work就是一个能长时间运行、能处理多步骤复杂任务、能自主做决策的AI员工。

举几个它能做的事：你让它"分析一下我们过去三个月的销售数据，找出下滑的原因，做一份PPT报告"——它会自己去数据库里取数据、自己分析、自己找原因、自己做PPT，最后把成品交给你。中间不需要你一步一步指导。

再比如：你让它"把这个客户的所有邮件整理一下，提取关键信息，生成一份客户画像，然后写一封回复邮件"——它会自己去翻邮件、自己总结、自己分析、自己写邮件，你只需要最后看一眼、点个发送。

这和以前的ChatGPT有什么不一样？以前的ChatGPT是"助手"——你说一步，它做一步，你得全程盯着，错了要纠正，跑偏了要拉回来。现在的ChatGPT Work是"同事"——你把任务交给它，它自己想办法完成，中间遇到问题可能会来问你，但大部分时候它自己搞定。

支撑这个能力的，是GPT-5.6 Ultra的多Agent模式。简单说就是：一个任务来了，不是一个大模型从头干到尾，而是系统自动拆分任务、调用多个专门的Agent、分工协作、最后汇总结果。就像一个团队一样，有做分析的、有写代码的、有做设计的、有写文案的，大家配合起来把一件事做完。

企业市场的新战场

ChatGPT Work的发布，意味着OpenAI正式把战火烧到了企业级市场。

以前OpenAI的收入主要靠什么？靠个人订阅（ChatGPT Plus）、靠API调用。个人订阅用户基数大，但ARPU低；API收入不错，但客户留存不稳定——今天你模型最强大家用你，明天别人出了个更便宜的大家就跑了。

企业级市场就不一样了。一旦一个企业把工作流建在你的平台上、把数据接进来、把员工培训好，迁移成本就会非常高。这是一个粘性极强、ARPU极高、生命周期价值极高的市场。以前这个市场是微软的天下——Microsoft 365 Copilot背靠Office生态，几乎没有对手。

现在OpenAI亲自下场了。

ChatGPT Work vs Microsoft 365 Copilot，这会是接下来两年企业级AI市场最精彩的对决。一边是最强的模型能力和最前沿的Agent技术，一边是最成熟的办公生态和最广泛的企业客户。两边各有优势，也各有软肋。

OpenAI的优势是模型和Agent能力领先，产品迭代快，没有历史包袱。劣势是企业服务经验不足，没有办公软件基座，安全和合规能力需要补课。

微软的优势是Office生态牢不可破，企业客户关系深厚，安全合规体系完善。劣势是产品迭代慢，Agent能力不如OpenAI灵活，内部组织协调成本高。

这场对决的结果，现在还很难说。但有一点是确定的：企业级AI Agent市场，会是接下来AI行业最大的增量市场。谁能拿下这个市场，谁就是AI时代的下一个王者。

从对话到工作，从工具到同事，从C端到B端——OpenAI的每一步都踩在产业演进的节点上。ChatGPT Work只是一个开始，真正的好戏还在后面。

明天见。

On July 10, OpenAI dropped two big things at once: the GPT-5.6 model family, and ChatGPT Work.

Most people's attention gravitated toward GPT-5.6 — three tiers (Sol/Terra/Luna), Ultra multi-agent mode, capability improvements here and there. But in my view, the actually important announcement is ChatGPT Work.

Because it marks a strategic pivot for OpenAI: from "conversational AI" to "work AI". The old ChatGPT was you-ask-it-answers, one question one response. ChatGPT Work is you give it a task, and it plans on its own, executes on its own, gets it done on its own — working continuously like a real employee.

This isn't a difference in degree. It's a paradigm shift.

What Actually Is ChatGPT Work?

The official definition is "enterprise-grade agent," but that's too abstract. In plain English: ChatGPT Work is an AI employee that can run for extended periods, handle complex multi-step tasks, and make autonomous decisions.

A few examples of what it can do: you tell it "analyze our sales data from the past three months, find the reasons for the decline, and make a PowerPoint report" — it pulls data from the database on its own, analyzes on its own, finds root causes on its own, builds the deck on its own, and hands you the finished product. No step-by-step guidance needed from you.

Another example: you tell it "go through all this customer's emails, extract key information, generate a customer profile, then write a reply email" — it digs through emails on its own, summarizes on its own, analyzes on its own, writes the email on its own. You just take a final look and hit send.

What's the difference from old ChatGPT? Old ChatGPT was an "assistant" — you say one step, it does one step, you have to watch the whole time, correct it when it's wrong, pull it back when it goes off track. ChatGPT Work is a "colleague" — you hand it a task, it figures out how to get it done. It might come to you with questions if it hits issues, but most of the time it handles things itself.

Powering this capability is GPT-5.6 Ultra's multi-agent mode. Simply put: when a task comes in, it's not one big model doing everything from start to finish. The system automatically breaks down the task, calls multiple specialized agents, they collaborate by division of labor, and finally aggregate results. Just like a team — some do analysis, some write code, some do design, some write copy — everyone works together to get the job done.

A New Battleground in the Enterprise Market

The launch of ChatGPT Work means OpenAI is officially bringing the fight to the enterprise market.

What did OpenAI's revenue mainly come from before? Individual subscriptions (ChatGPT Plus) and API calls. Individual subscriptions have large user bases but low ARPU. API revenue is decent, but customer retention is unstable — today your model is strongest and everyone uses you; tomorrow someone cheaper comes along and everyone leaves.

The enterprise market is different. Once a company builds its workflows on your platform, connects its data, trains its employees — the migration cost becomes extremely high. It's a market with incredibly strong stickiness, extremely high ARPU, and extremely high lifetime value. Before, this market was Microsoft's world — Microsoft 365 Copilot, backed by the Office ecosystem, had virtually no competition.

Now OpenAI is entering the ring directly.

ChatGPT Work vs. Microsoft 365 Copilot — this will be the most exciting showdown in enterprise AI over the next two years. On one side, the strongest model capabilities and cutting-edge agent technology. On the other, the most mature office ecosystem and the broadest enterprise customer base. Each side has its advantages; each side has its weaknesses.

OpenAI's advantages: leading model and agent capabilities, fast product iteration, no legacy baggage. Disadvantages: insufficient enterprise services experience, no office software foundation, safety and compliance capabilities need catching up.

Microsoft's advantages: the unbreakable Office ecosystem, deep enterprise customer relationships, mature safety and compliance systems. Disadvantages: slow product iteration, agent capabilities less flexible than OpenAI's, high internal coordination costs.

The outcome of this showdown is hard to call right now. But one thing is certain: the enterprise AI agent market will be the biggest growth market in the AI industry going forward. Whoever wins this market becomes the next king of the AI era.

From conversation to work, from tool to colleague, from consumer to enterprise — every step OpenAI takes lands on an inflection point of industry evolution. ChatGPT Work is just the beginning. The real show is still ahead.

See you tomorrow.
