72小时。

6月9日，Anthropic无预警发布两款顶级模型——面向公众的Claude Fable 5和面向特定B端合作方的Claude Mythos 5。两者共享底层权重，仅安全护栏不同，在多项基准测试中刷新纪录。

72小时后的6月12日，白宫以国家安全为由、担忧模型被越狱用于恶意目的，对Fable 5和Mythos 5下达了出口管制令。

这是人类历史上第一次，一个AI模型在发布后不到三天就被政府以战略物资级别进行出口管控。这件事的历史意义，怎么强调都不为过。

为什么是72小时

出口管制来得如此之快，说明美国政府对Anthropic新模型的能力评估早有准备。Fable 5是首款面向公众的Mythos级别模型——"Mythos"是Anthropic内部对最高能力等级模型的命名，此前从未对公众开放。

管制的核心理由是"越狱风险"：美国政府担心Mythos级别的模型一旦被开源或被境外实体获得，可能被用于网络攻击、生物武器设计、大规模虚假信息生成等高危场景。但更深层的原因是前沿AI能力的代差优势——当模型能力跨过某个阈值，它就不再仅仅是一个商业产品，而是国家竞争力的核心组成部分。

加拿大总理Mark Carney随后公开警告过度依赖少数美国AI供应商的风险。印度舆论也将此视为长期依赖美国AI基础设施的警示信号。这两个国家的反应说明：美国的出口管制不仅没有稳住盟友，反而加速了全球AI供应链的"去美国化"思考。

硅谷三巨头的意外同盟

更值得关注的是另一个信号：Anthropic就模型蒸馏问题起诉阿里巴巴，并透露已与OpenAI、Google共享违规蒸馏案例库。这是三家在美国AI议程上竞争激烈的公司，首次在知识产权保护和出口管制上形成实质性同盟。

这个同盟的出现传递了一个明确信号：美国AI三巨头在"对外防御"上利益一致。无论它们之间在商业上如何争夺市场份额，在防止中国等竞争对手通过蒸馏、逆向工程等方式缩小模型能力差距这件事上，它们站在了同一边。

埃隆·马斯克在X平台反驳称"Anthropic自身犯有大规模窃取训练数据行为"，但这种声音在硅谷主流叙事中已经被边缘化。

Anthropic出口管制事件的长期影响，将是加速全球AI市场的分裂。

企业和各国政府将不得不面对一个现实：依赖美国的前沿AI模型存在政策风险——今天你能用，明天一纸管制令你就用不了。这会推动三个趋势：欧盟加速发展本土AI能力、印度等人口大国加大AI自主化投入、中国继续走独立技术路线。

对中国AI公司来说，这既是压力也是动力。压力在于获取最前沿模型能力的窗口被进一步收紧；动力在于，当全球客户意识到单一供应商风险时，不依赖美国技术栈的替代方案将获得更大的市场空间。DeepSeek在国产芯片上完成全参数训练，就在这个大背景下显得尤为珍贵。

前沿AI的"巴尔干化"已经不可逆转。未来的全球AI版图，不会是一个互联互通的平的世界，而是几个大的技术生态圈并存竞争的格局。

72 hours.

On June 9, Anthropic surprise-launched two top-tier models -- Claude Fable 5 for the public and Claude Mythos 5 for select B2B partners. They share underlying weights, differing only in safety guardrails, and broke records across multiple benchmarks.

72 hours later on June 12, the White House issued an export control order on Fable 5 and Mythos 5 on national security grounds, citing concerns that the models could be jailbroken for malicious purposes.

This is the first time in human history that an AI model has been placed under strategic-materials-level export controls less than three days after launch. The historical significance of this event can hardly be overstated.

Why 72 Hours?

The export controls came so fast that they indicate the U.S. government had already prepared assessments of Anthropic's new model capabilities. Fable 5 is the first Mythos-level model released to the public -- "Mythos" is Anthropic's internal designation for its highest capability tier, never before opened to the public.

The core justification for controls is "jailbreak risk": the U.S. government fears that if Mythos-level models were open-sourced or obtained by foreign entities, they could be used for cyberattacks, bioweapons design, large-scale disinformation generation, and other high-risk scenarios. But the deeper reason is the generational capability gap in frontier AI -- when model capability crosses a certain threshold, it ceases to be merely a commercial product and becomes a core component of national competitiveness.

Canadian Prime Minister Mark Carney subsequently publicly warned about the risks of over-reliance on a handful of American AI providers. Indian commentary also framed this as a warning signal about long-term dependence on U.S. AI infrastructure. The reactions from these two countries show that U.S. export controls haven't reassured allies -- they've accelerated global thinking about "de-Americanization" of AI supply chains.

The Unexpected Alliance of Silicon Valley's Big Three

Another signal deserves more attention: Anthropic sued Alibaba over model distillation, and revealed it has shared a database of violating distillation cases with OpenAI and Google. This is the first time these three companies -- fierce competitors on the U.S. AI agenda -- have formed a substantive alliance on IP protection and export controls.

The emergence of this alliance sends a clear signal: America's AI big three are aligned on "external defense." However they compete for market share commercially, they stand on the same side when it comes to preventing competitors like China from narrowing the model capability gap through distillation, reverse engineering, or similar means.

Elon Musk retorted on X that "Anthropic itself is guilty of massive training data theft," but such voices have been marginalized in mainstream Silicon Valley narratives.

"Chips used to be strategic materials. Now model weights are too. The 72-hour control order marks AI officially entering national security discourse at the same level as nuclear weapons and chips." -- A Washington think tank researcher's comment

The Balkanization of Global AI Markets

The long-term impact of the Anthropic export control incident will be accelerating fragmentation of the global AI market.

Enterprises and governments will have to confront a reality: depending on U.S. frontier AI models carries policy risk -- you can use them today, but a single control order tomorrow could cut you off. This will drive three trends: the EU accelerating development of domestic AI capabilities, populous nations like India increasing investment in AI sovereignty, and China continuing its independent technology path.

For Chinese AI companies, this is both pressure and motivation. Pressure: the window for accessing the most frontier model capabilities is further tightened. Motivation: when global customers recognize single-vendor risk, alternatives that don't depend on the U.S. tech stack gain greater market space. DeepSeek completing full-parameter training on domestic chips looks especially valuable against this backdrop.

The "Balkanization" of frontier AI is irreversible. The future global AI landscape won't be a flat, interconnected world, but one where several large technology ecosystems coexist and compete.
