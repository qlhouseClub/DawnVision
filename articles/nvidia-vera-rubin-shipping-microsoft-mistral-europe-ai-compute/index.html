Blackwell还没捂热，Rubin就来了。

2026年7月，英伟达新一代GPU架构Vera Rubin开始向北美主要云提供商发货，标志着AI算力正式从Blackwell时代迈入Rubin时代。首个公开的大规模Rubin部署计划来自一个意想不到的组合：微软与法国大模型独角兽Mistral AI签署数十亿欧元合作协议，计划在法国本土部署数千颗Vera Rubin GPU，打造欧洲首个吉瓦级AI算力集群。

为什么是欧洲？为什么是Mistral？

这笔交易的政治意义不亚于技术意义。

过去两年，欧洲在AI基础设施上的焦虑感与日俱增。前沿大模型的训练和推理算力几乎全部集中在美国——微软、Google、Amazon、Meta的数据中心承载了全球大部分AI工作负载。欧洲虽然有Mistral这样的本土明星公司，但Mistral的模型训练严重依赖美国云厂商的GPU资源，这在数据主权和产业自主的话语体系下是一个政治软肋。

微软与Mistral的合作直接回应了这个痛点：在欧洲本土、用最新一代GPU、建吉瓦级算力集群。这不是几百张卡的小规模实验，这是真正能支撑前沿模型训练和大规模推理的基础设施。法国政府一直积极推动“AI主权”战略，这笔交易可以被包装成欧洲AI自主可控的标志性成果。

对微软而言，这是一步精明的战略棋。通过与Mistral合作，微软在欧洲AI基础设施市场抢下了一个锚点客户，同时向欧洲监管机构展示“我不是来垄断的，我是来赋能本土生态的”。对Mistral而言，获得微软的资金和基础设施支持，意味着它可以继续在前沿模型竞赛中与OpenAI、Anthropic掰手腕，而不必完全依赖美国三大云的施舍。

Rubin时代意味着什么？

Vera Rubin是英伟达继Blackwell之后的下一代GPU架构。虽然具体技术参数尚未完全公开，但从供应链和早期客户信息来看，Rubin在算力密度、能效比和互连带宽上相比Blackwell有显著提升，重点优化了推理工作负载——这与AI产业从“练模型”到“用模型”的重心转移高度吻合。

值得注意的是，“数千颗”Vera Rubin的单看体量并不算特别大——对比微软在美国本土数十万张GPU的部署规模，几千颗只是一个起点。真正拉动供应链预期的不是这笔订单的绝对量，而是它的信号意义：Rubin已经从“下一代产品”变成了“现在可以下单的产品”。云厂商、大模型公司、主权AI项目的采购决策会开始围绕Rubin而非Blackwell展开。

这对中国AI产业也有间接影响。美国对华GPU出口管制仍在持续，Rubin的高端版本几乎确定不会进入中国市场。但中国的国产替代正在加速——阿里平头哥在WAIC上开源了SAIL软件栈，真武芯片累计出货已达56万片。全球AI算力市场正在分化为两个体系：北美体系（英伟达+三大云）和中国体系（国产芯片+国产软件栈），欧洲则试图在两者之间走第三条路。

明天见。

Blackwell hasn't even cooled off, and Rubin is already here.

In July 2026, NVIDIA's next-generation GPU architecture Vera Rubin began shipping to major North American cloud providers, marking AI compute's official transition from the Blackwell era to the Rubin era. The first publicly announced large-scale Rubin deployment comes from an unexpected pairing: Microsoft signed a multi-billion-euro cooperation agreement with French LLM unicorn Mistral AI, planning to deploy thousands of Vera Rubin GPUs in France itself to build Europe's first gigawatt-scale AI compute cluster.

Why Europe? Why Mistral?

This deal's political significance rivals its technical significance.

Over the past two years, Europe's anxiety about AI infrastructure has intensified steadily. Frontier LLM training and inference compute are almost entirely concentrated in the U.S. — Microsoft, Google, Amazon, and Meta's data centers carry most of the world's AI workloads. Europe has homegrown stars like Mistral, but Mistral's model training relies heavily on American cloud vendors' GPU resources, a political soft target in the discourse around data sovereignty and industrial autonomy.

Microsoft and Mistral's partnership directly addresses this pain point: on European soil, using the latest generation of GPUs, building a gigawatt-scale compute cluster. This isn't a few-hundred-card experiment; this is genuine infrastructure capable of supporting frontier model training and large-scale inference. The French government has been actively pushing an “AI sovereignty” strategy, and this deal can be packaged as a landmark win for European AI autonomy.

For Microsoft, this is a shrewd strategic move. Partnering with Mistral gives Microsoft an anchor customer in the European AI infrastructure market while signaling to European regulators that “I'm not here to monopolize; I'm here to empower the local ecosystem.” For Mistral, Microsoft's financial and infrastructure backing means it can continue competing with OpenAI and Anthropic in the frontier model race without being entirely dependent on the whims of the big three U.S. cloud providers.

What Does the Rubin Era Mean?

Vera Rubin is NVIDIA's next GPU architecture after Blackwell. While full technical specs haven't been publicly disclosed, supply chain and early customer information indicate Rubin delivers significant improvements over Blackwell in compute density, energy efficiency, and interconnect bandwidth, with a particular focus on optimizing for inference workloads — aligning perfectly with the AI industry's center of gravity shifting from “training models” to “running models.”

Notably, “thousands” of Vera Rubin GPUs isn't massive in absolute terms — compared to Microsoft's hundreds of thousands of GPUs deployed in the U.S., a few thousand is just a starting point. What really moves supply chain expectations isn't this order's absolute size but its signaling value: Rubin has moved from “next-gen product” to “orderable product now.” Procurement decisions at cloud providers, LLM companies, and sovereign AI projects will begin to orbit Rubin rather than Blackwell.

This has indirect implications for China's AI industry. U.S. GPU export controls to China remain in place, and high-end Rubin versions will almost certainly be barred from the Chinese market. But China's domestic substitution is accelerating — Alibaba's T-Head open-sourced its SAIL software stack at WAIC, with Zhenwu chips shipping a cumulative 560,000 units. The global AI compute market is bifurcating into two systems: the North American system (NVIDIA + big three clouds) and the Chinese system (domestic chips + domestic software stack), while Europe attempts a third path between the two.
