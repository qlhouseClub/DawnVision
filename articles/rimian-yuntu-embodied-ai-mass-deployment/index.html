WAIC 2026的现场，具身智能终于不再是跳舞视频和后空翻Demo的舞台。日冕与远图联合宣布万台级具身智能部署计划，物理AI的闭环第一次有人宣称跑通了。

万台部署意味着什么

按照规划：日冕和远图将首先在服务器制造场景验证"超级工站"能力，2027年完成百台级部署，未来实现万台级具身智能产品部署。

这不是实验室里的PR稿，这是工厂里的真金白银。服务器制造是一个对精度、稳定性、良率要求极高的场景——比在舞台上做后空翻难100倍。如果具身智能能在服务器制造场景跑通，意味着它已经跨过了"能用"到"好用"的门槛。

同一天在WAIC现场，酷哇科技也展示了行业首个双层智能体世界模型——机器人需要的不是单一物理世界模型，而是物理世界模型与人类社会世界模型的统一。这个观点非常关键：机器人不是在真空里工作，它要理解人类的规则、习惯、协作方式。

王兴兴的2-3年预测

7月22日，世界互联网大会数字丝路发展论坛在西安举行。宇树科技创始人王兴兴在开幕式上给出了一个明确的时间预测：具身智能的"ChatGPT时刻"有望最快在两三年内到来。

什么是具身智能的ChatGPT时刻？王兴兴的定义是："届时机器人在大部分陌生场景，也能直接工作实现很多基本功能。"

翻译成人话：现在你买一台人形机器人，它可能在你家里连一杯水都倒不利索，因为你家的布局、杯子的位置、地板的材质都是它没见过的。ChatGPT时刻意味着：机器人进入一个全新环境，不需要针对每个场景重新编程，它能像人一样快速适应，直接干活。

王兴兴说这话是有底气的：宇树是目前全球人形机器人出货量最大的公司之一，他们的机器人已经在多个真实场景部署。

At WAIC 2026, embodied intelligence finally moved beyond backflip demos and dance videos. Rimian and Yuntu jointly announced a 10,000-unit embodied AI deployment plan — the first claimed closed-loop for physical AI.

What 10,000 Units Actually Means

According to the plan: Rimian and Yuntu will first validate "super workstation" capabilities in server manufacturing, achieve 100-unit deployment in 2027, and scale to 10,000+ units of embodied AI products in the future.

This isn't a lab press release — this is real money on factory floors. Server manufacturing is a scenario with extremely high requirements for precision, stability, and yield — 100x harder than doing backflips on stage. If embodied AI can work in server manufacturing, it has crossed the threshold from "usable" to "useful."

Also at WAIC, Kuwa Technology showcased the industry's first dual-layer agent world model — robots don't need just a physical world model, but the unification of physical world models and human social world models. This is a crucial insight: robots don't work in a vacuum; they need to understand human rules, habits, and collaboration patterns.

Wang Xingxing's 2-3 Year Prediction

On July 22, at the World Internet Conference Digital Silk Road Forum in Xi'an, Unitree Robotics founder Wang Xingxing gave a concrete timeline: the "ChatGPT moment" for embodied AI will arrive within 2-3 years, at the earliest.

What is the ChatGPT moment for embodied AI? Wang's definition: "A point where robots can directly work and perform many basic functions even in most unfamiliar scenarios."

In plain language: right now, if you buy a humanoid robot, it might struggle to pour a glass of water in your home, because your layout, cup positions, and flooring are all unfamiliar. The ChatGPT moment means: a robot enters a completely new environment, no per-scene reprogramming needed, it adapts quickly like a human and just does the job.

Wang has credibility here: Unitree is one of the world's largest humanoid robot manufacturers by shipment volume, with robots already deployed in multiple real-world scenarios.

Embodied AI in 2026: From Stunts to Deployment

Looking at embodied AI news from H1 2026, a clear shift emerges:

At the start of the year, everyone was competing on whose robot could do cooler backflips or run marathons. By mid-year, the news shifted to who got automotive orders, who entered factories, who achieved small-batch delivery. Hyundai's full acquisition of Boston Dynamics, Yuequan Bionic's 10,000-unit dexterous hand funding, Horizon's VW partnership, Rimian-Yuntu's 10K plan — all pointing the same direction: embodied AI's capital story is becoming an industrial story.

This shift looks a lot like LLMs in 2023: in the beginning everyone compared parameter counts; by year-end everyone compared revenue, paying customers, and deployment scenarios.

Two to three years sounds fast, but that's how industrial timelines work — once technology crosses a threshold, penetration moves faster than anyone expects. Just as LLMs suddenly exploded in 2023, embodied AI's explosion could catch everyone off guard.

The question isn't "will embodied AI come?" — it's "are you prepared for when it does?"
