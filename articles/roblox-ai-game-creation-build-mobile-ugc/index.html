做一款3D游戏需要多久？Roblox的答案是：一句话的时间。

7月28日，Roblox将在移动端正式上线「Build」功能——用户打开App，输入一段文字描述，AI就能生成一款可以直接玩的3D游戏。不是概念图，不是Demo，是有场景、有角色、有交互、有规则的完整小游戏。

这件事的分量，怎么高估都不为过。在Roblox上，以前做一款能玩的游戏，你得会Lua编程、会3D建模、会关卡设计——门槛不低。现在，门槛被AI一脚踹碎了。

从「做游戏」到「描述游戏」，中间隔了多少创作者

Roblox现在有多少创作者？官方数字是几百万。听着很多，但你想想Roblox的月活用户——超过7亿。几百万创作者对7亿用户，比例大概是1:1000。

为什么这么少？因为做游戏难啊。你得学编程、得学建模、得懂游戏设计，还要有时间反复调试。绝大多数玩家就是「玩游戏的」，不是「做游戏的」。

Build功能出来之后，这个比例会变成多少？没人知道，但肯定不是1:1000了。可能是1:100，可能是1:10，甚至可能更高——当创作的门槛降到零，每个人都是创作者。

这不是第一次发生这种事了。当年YouTube把视频创作的门槛从「会用专业摄像机+剪辑软件」降到「有个手机就行」，结果是创作者数量爆炸式增长，内容生态彻底变了天。Roblox现在做的，就是游戏界的YouTube时刻。

但AI生成的游戏，好玩吗？

一个必须面对的问题：AI生成的游戏，质量到底怎么样？

从目前泄露的测试视频来看，Build生成的游戏还比较简单——基本是跑酷、解谜、收集类的小游戏，场景复杂度有限，玩法也比较单一。跟专业团队做的Roblox游戏比，差得还远。

但这重要吗？不重要。因为大多数人做游戏不是为了做爆款，是为了跟朋友分享。你给你女朋友做一个「一起闯关收集爱心」的小游戏，哪怕画面粗糙、玩法简单，她也会觉得你浪漫。你给你的同学做一个「考试答题闯关」的游戏，哪怕逻辑简单，大家也会玩得很嗨。

"AI生成的内容不需要完美，只需要够特别——特别到你愿意分享给朋友。"—— 一位UGC平台产品经理

而且质量会快速提升的。今天生成的是简单跑酷，明年生成的可能就是完整的RPG了。AI的进步速度，比人类学习编程的速度快太多了。

真正值得关注的不是「AI能不能做出好游戏」，而是「当每个人都能做游戏，平台上会出现什么样的新内容生态」。是垃圾内容泛滥？还是百花齐放？是头部效应更强？还是长尾更丰富？这些问题，只有等Build真正上线之后才能有答案。

但有一件事是确定的：游戏创作的民主化，已经不可逆了。

明天见。

How long does it take to make a 3D game? Roblox's answer: as long as it takes to type a sentence.

On July 28, Roblox will officially launch its "Build" feature on mobile — users open the app, type a text description, and AI generates a fully playable 3D game. Not concept art, not a demo — a complete mini-game with environments, characters, interactions, and rules.

The significance of this can't be overstated. On Roblox, making a playable game used to require knowing Lua programming, 3D modeling, and level design — a high barrier. Now, AI has kicked that barrier to pieces.

From "Making Games" to "Describing Games" — How Many Creators Are in Between?

How many creators does Roblox have right now? The official number is a few million. Sounds like a lot — until you consider Roblox's monthly active users: over 700 million. A few million creators to 700 million users — roughly a 1:1000 ratio.

Why so few? Because making games is hard. You have to learn programming, learn modeling, understand game design, and have time to iterate and debug. Most players are just "people who play games," not "people who make games."

After Build launches, what will that ratio become? Nobody knows — but it definitely won't be 1:1000. Maybe 1:100, maybe 1:10, maybe even higher. When the creation barrier drops to zero, everyone is a creator.

This isn't the first time this has happened. When YouTube lowered video creation from "professional camera + editing software" to "just a phone," the result was an explosion in creator count and a complete transformation of the content ecosystem. What Roblox is doing now is the YouTube moment for gaming.

But Are AI-Generated Games Any Fun?

A question that must be faced: how good are AI-generated games, really?

From leaked test videos so far, Build generates relatively simple games — basically parkour, puzzle, and collection-type mini-games, with limited scene complexity and straightforward gameplay. Compared to games made by professional Roblox teams, there's still a big gap.

But does that matter? No. Because most people don't make games to create hits — they make them to share with friends. You make a "collect hearts together" game for your girlfriend — even if the graphics are rough and gameplay is simple, she'll think it's romantic. You make an "exam question闯关" game for your classmates — even if the logic is basic, everyone will have a blast playing it.

"AI-generated content doesn't need to be perfect — it just needs to be special enough that you want to share it with friends."— A UGC platform product manager

And quality will improve fast. Today it generates simple parkour. Next year it might generate full RPGs. AI progresses much faster than humans learn to code.

What's really worth watching isn't "can AI make good games" — it's "when everyone can make games, what kind of new content ecosystem emerges on the platform." Will garbage content flood everything? Or will we see a thousand flowers bloom? Will the head get bigger or the long tail richer? These questions can only be answered after Build actually launches.

But one thing is certain: the democratization of game creation is irreversible.
