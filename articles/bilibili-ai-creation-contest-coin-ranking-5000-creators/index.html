5000人。这是B站AI创造公开赛开赛一个月的参赛创作者数量。

B站正式为AI创造公开赛上线了投币榜功能——观众可以给参赛的AI作品投币，榜单根据投币数实时排名，第一名的作品将获得首页推荐和流量扶持。这个功能上线后，AI创作区的日活直接上涨了40%，很多以前只看不发的用户也开始动手做AI作品了。

这个数字值得注意。去年这个时候，AI绘画、AI视频还是小圈子里的玩具——会用Stable Diffusion、能跑通SVD的人被称为"大佬"。仅仅一年时间，AI创作工具的门槛已经降到了普通用户也能上手的程度，B站一个比赛就能吸引5000人参赛，说明AI内容创作真的进入全民时代了。

投币榜解决了什么问题？

B站为什么要给AI比赛单独做一个投币榜？因为AI内容面临一个很尴尬的问题：质量参差不齐，观众不知道什么值得看。

AI创作的门槛降低是好事，但副作用也很明显：垃圾内容指数级增长。以前AI生成的视频一眼就能看出来——手是畸形的、画面闪烁、逻辑不通；现在AI生成质量越来越高，普通观众很难分辨哪些是真正花了心思的好作品，哪些是随便输入几个prompt跑出来的流水线产物。

投币榜的本质是用社区投票代替算法推荐。B站的投币是一个比点赞更重的行为——用户每天投币数量有限，只会给自己真正喜欢的作品投币。用投币数排名，相当于让社区用真金白银（虚拟货币）给AI作品投票，比单纯的播放量、点赞量更能筛选出真正高质量的内容。

而且投币榜还有一个好处：给创作者一个正反馈循环。以前做AI作品发B站，可能播放量几百、评论几条，创作者不知道自己做得好不好；现在有了榜单，看到自己的作品上榜、被投币，会有继续创作的动力。这对于培育一个健康的AI创作社区来说非常重要。

AI重构UGC生态：不是替代创作者，是降低创作门槛

很多人担心AI会替代内容创作者，B站这个比赛给出了相反的答案：AI不是替代创作者，是让更多人成为创作者。

我们看这次参赛的5000个创作者，很多人的身份不是"画师""UP主""视频制作人"——他们是学生、上班族、医生、老师、程序员，以前没有专业技能做视频/画画，但有了AI工具之后，他们可以把自己的创意变成内容发出来。参赛作品里有高中生用AI做的科幻短片，有医生用AI做的医学科普动画，有程序员用AI做的编程教程——这些内容以前根本不会存在，因为这些人没有专业制作能力。

"最好的创作工具，不是让专业创作者做得更快，是让以前不会创作的人也能开始创作。"—— 一位内容平台产品负责人

UGC平台的本质是什么？不是少数专业创作者生产内容给大多数人看，是每个人都能生产内容给其他人看。从文字（微博、知乎）到图片（小红书、Instagram）到短视频（抖音、B站），每一次媒介升级，都伴随着创作门槛的降低，带来内容数量的爆炸式增长。AI就是下一次媒介升级——它把视频、动画、3D这些以前需要专业技能的创作形式，门槛降到了普通人也能上手的程度。

当然，问题也会随之而来：版权怎么算？AI生成内容的归属权是谁？怎么防止低俗、暴力、深度伪造内容？这些问题都还没有答案，需要平台、监管、创作者一起摸索。但方向是确定的：创作会变得越来越容易，好内容永远稀缺，真正有创意、有审美、有观点的创作者，在AI时代只会更值钱——因为工具人人都有，但审美和创意是学不来的。

明天见。

5,000. That's how many creators have entered Bilibili's AI Creation Contest in its first month.

Bilibili has officially launched the Coin Leaderboard feature for its AI Creation Contest — viewers can award coins to participating AI works, the ranking updates in real-time based on coins, and the top work gets homepage placement and traffic support. After the feature launched, daily active users in the AI creation zone jumped 40%, and many users who previously only lurked started making AI works themselves.

This number is noteworthy. This time last year, AI art and AI video were still niche toys — people who could use Stable Diffusion or run SVD were called "experts." Just one year later, AI creation tool barriers have dropped low enough for mainstream users, and a single contest on Bilibili drawing 5,000 participants shows AI content creation has truly entered the mass-market era.

What Problem Does the Coin Leaderboard Solve?

Why did Bilibili build a dedicated coin leaderboard for an AI contest? Because AI content faces an awkward problem: uneven quality makes it hard for viewers to know what's worth watching.

Lower barriers to AI creation are good, but the side effect is obvious: garbage content grows exponentially. Early AI-generated videos were instantly recognizable — deformed hands, flickering frames, nonsensical logic; now AI generation quality keeps improving, and ordinary viewers can't easily tell which works had real effort put in versus which were assembly-line products from typing a few prompts.

The Coin Leaderboard's essence is using community voting instead of algorithmic recommendations. Bilibili's coins are a heavier action than likes — users have a limited daily coin supply and only award them to works they genuinely love. Ranking by coins is essentially letting the community vote with real (virtual) money for AI works, filtering out genuine quality better than simple view counts or likes.

The Coin Leaderboard has another benefit: it gives creators a positive feedback loop. Previously, posting an AI work on Bilibili might get a few hundred views and a couple of comments, leaving creators unsure if they were doing well; with a leaderboard, seeing their work ranked and awarded coins creates motivation to keep creating. This is critical for nurturing a healthy AI creation community.

AI Reshaping the UGC Ecosystem: Not Replacing Creators — Lowering Barriers

Many worry AI will replace content creators, but Bilibili's contest suggests the opposite: AI doesn't replace creators — it lets more people become creators.

Looking at the 5,000 creators in this contest, many don't identify as "artists," "UP hosts," or "video producers" — they're students, office workers, doctors, teachers, programmers who previously lacked the professional skills to make videos or art, but with AI tools can now turn their ideas into content and publish them. Entries include sci-fi shorts made by high schoolers with AI, medical education animations by doctors, programming tutorials by programmers — content that would never have existed before, because these people didn't have professional production skills.

"The best creation tools don't just make professional creators faster — they let people who could never create before start creating."— A content platform product lead

What's the essence of UGC platforms? It's not a handful of professional creators producing content for the masses — it's everyone being able to produce content for everyone else. From text (Weibo, Zhihu) to images (Xiaohongshu, Instagram) to short video (Douyin, Bilibili), every medium upgrade brings lower creation barriers and explosive growth in content volume. AI is the next medium upgrade — it lowers barriers for video, animation, 3D, and other formats that previously required professional skills to the point where ordinary people can use them.

Of course, questions come with it: who owns the copyright? Who holds rights to AI-generated content? How do we prevent vulgar, violent, or deepfake content? These questions don't have answers yet — they need platforms, regulators, and creators figuring them out together. But the direction is certain: creation will keep getting easier, good content will always be scarce, and creators with genuine originality, taste, and perspective will only become more valuable in the AI age — because anyone can use tools, but taste and creativity can't be taught.
