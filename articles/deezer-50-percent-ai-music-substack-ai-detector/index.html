Deezer: 50% Daily Uploads AI Music
The Flood Arrives, Platforms Build Dams

Deezer数据显示6月日均9万首AI音乐上传，占比首次突破50%。Bandcamp封禁、Tidal断 monetization、Substack上线AI检测器——内容平台的态度从拥抱转向防御。

Deezer reports 90,000 AI-generated tracks uploaded daily in June, crossing 50% for the first time. Bandcamp bans, Tidal demonetizes, Substack launches AI detector — content platforms shift from embrace to defense.

No.019 2026.07.22 约 5 分钟阅读 ~5 min read

Deezer的最新数据给整个内容行业敲了警钟：2026年6月，平台日均上传9万首AI生成音乐，占日上传总量的比例首次突破50%。

这不是未来预测，这是已经发生的事实。你每天听到的新歌里，一半以上可能是AI写的。

从10%到50%只用了18个月

回看Deezer的数据曲线，这个增长速度令人咋舌：

2025年1月：日均1万首，占比10%；2025年4月：2万首，18%；2025年9月：3万首，28%；2025年11月：5万首，34%；2026年1月：6万首，39%；2026年4月：7.5万首，44%；2026年6月：9万首，50%+。

指数增长。而且这只是音乐一个品类。文字、图片、视频的情况只会更夸张。

Deezer CEO Alexis Lanternier说："近两年我们一直在打击欺诈和减少AI音乐相关的支付稀释。现在每日上传一半是AI生成曲目，我们正在采取额外措施保护艺术家和词曲作者的权益。"

Deezer的新措施包括：下架6个月内没有播放量的AI曲目，清理刷量欺诈内容。

平台态度的集体转向

值得注意的是，不只是Deezer，几乎所有内容平台在2026年都开始对AI内容收紧政策：

Bandcamp直接封禁AI生成音乐，态度最激进；Tidal切断AI音乐的monetization（变现通道）；Apple Music推出自愿AI标签系统；Spotify更新政策规定AI音乐使用程度并打击垃圾内容；YouTube本周刚更新政策，明确定义不能获得广告收入的AI垃圾内容类型；Substack7月22日上线AI检测器，可以扫描帖子、笔记、回复、评论，估计文本中有多少是AI生成的。

平台的态度从2023年的"AI是未来，拥抱它"，变成了2026年的"AI是洪水，我们需要建堤坝"。

内容稀缺性的反转

这背后是一个根本性的供需反转：

在互联网前50年，内容平台的核心矛盾是"内容不够多，用户没东西看"；从2026年开始，核心矛盾变成了"内容太多了，用户找不到真正有价值的东西"。

当AI可以无限生产内容时，稀缺的不再是内容本身，而是人类的注意力和真实的人类创作。这也是为什么平台纷纷开始标注AI内容、下架低质量AI内容、给人类创作者更多曝光——因为平台最终卖的是用户体验，如果平台被AI垃圾淹没，用户就走了。

对于创作者来说，这意味着两件事：第一，"用AI堆量"的策略已经失效了，平台会识别并打压；第二，真正有个人风格、有真实情感、有人类体验的内容会越来越值钱。

50%只是开始。当这个比例达到90%的时候，那10%的人类创作会成为奢侈品。

明天见。

Deezer's latest numbers are a wake-up call for the entire content industry: in June 2026, the platform saw 90,000 AI-generated tracks uploaded daily, crossing 50% of total daily uploads for the first time.

This isn't a future prediction. This is what has already happened. More than half of the new tracks released each day could be written by AI.

From 10% to 50% in 18 Months

Looking at Deezer's growth curve, the pace is staggering:

January 2025: 10K tracks/day, 10%; April 2025: 20K, 18%; September 2025: 30K, 28%; November 2025: 50K, 34%; January 2026: 60K, 39%; April 2026: 75K, 44%; June 2026: 90K, 50%+.

Exponential growth. And this is just music. The picture for text, images, and video is almost certainly more dramatic.

"Deezer has been at the frontline of fighting fraud and reducing payment dilution related to AI music for almost two years," said CEO Alexis Lanternier. "Now that half of all daily uploads are AI-generated tracks, we are taking additional steps to safeguard the rights of artists and songwriters."

New measures include removing AI tracks with no streams in six months and purging fraudulent streaming activity.

A Collective Shift in Platform Stance

Notably, it's not just Deezer — nearly every content platform tightened AI content policies in 2026:

Bandcamp banned AI-generated music outright, the most aggressive stance; Tidal cut off monetization for AI music; Apple Music introduced voluntary AI tagging; Spotify updated policies around AI disclosure and spam; YouTube clarified exactly which categories of AI slop don't qualify for ad revenue; Substack launched an AI detector on July 22 that scans posts, notes, replies, and comments to estimate what percentage was AI-generated.

Platform sentiment shifted from "AI is the future, embrace it" in 2023 to "AI is a flood, we need dams" in 2026.

The Reversal of Content Scarcity

Behind this is a fundamental supply-demand inversion:

For the first 50 years of the internet, the core problem was "not enough content, users have nothing to consume." Starting in 2026, the core problem becomes "too much content, users can't find anything valuable."

When AI can produce infinite content, what becomes scarce isn't content itself — it's human attention and authentic human creation. That's why platforms are labeling AI content, removing low-quality AI material, and giving human creators more visibility — because platforms ultimately sell user experience, and if a platform is flooded with AI slop, users leave.

For creators, this means two things: first, the "churn out volume with AI" strategy is dead — platforms will detect and suppress it. Second, content with genuine personal voice, real emotion, and lived human experience will become increasingly valuable.

Fifty percent is just the beginning. When that number hits 90%, the 10% that's human-created will be luxury goods.

See you tomorrow.
