Figma的反击：代码层+Agent

6月25日，Figma发布了可能是其成立以来最大的一次更新。三个核心变化值得关注：

第一，代码层进入协作画布。团队可以直接在Figma中克隆代码仓库，在设计层测试流程，设计和开发之间的墙被进一步打通。这不是简单的"设计转代码"插件——而是代码本身成为设计画布的一部分，设计者和开发者在同一个空间里协作。

第二，Design Agent嵌入画布而非侧边栏。这是Figma AI策略最精妙的地方。之前的AI设计工具（包括Figma自己的早期AI功能）都是把ChatGPT类的对话框放在侧边栏——你描述需求，AI生成设计，你复制粘贴回来。这产生了巨大的"Toggle Tax"（切换成本）：频繁在设计工具和AI工具之间切换，上下文丢失，效率不升反降。Design Agent把懂设计系统的Agent直接嵌入画布，设计系统（组件库、Token、变量）成为AI的专属上下文窗口。设计系统越完善的团队，AI增益越大。

第三，Parallel Prompting和控制权优先。Agent可以同时跑出多个风格/方向方案供选择，但AI生成仅作为起点——Figma刻意强调"人做最终决策"，避免AI越俎代庖。这是对设计师群体心理的精准把握：设计师不怕AI帮忙，怕的是AI替他们做设计。

设计工具的范式转移

Figma vs Stitch的战争，本质上是两种设计工具哲学的碰撞。

Stitch代表的是"零门槛"路线：让不会设计的人也能用语音做出UI。它的目标用户不是专业设计师，而是产品经理、创业者、小企业主——那些有想法但不会用Figma的人。免费是它的武器，谷歌生态是它的分发渠道。

Figma代表的是"增强专业者"路线：让专业设计师和设计团队更高效。它的护城河是设计系统——企业的组件库、Token、变量、设计规范，这些是多年积累的组织资产，AI越深入理解这些资产，价值越大。

"Stitch要让人人都能做设计，Figma要让设计师变得更强大。这两个方向都成立，但商业逻辑完全不同——Stitch卖的是谷歌生态的粘性，Figma卖的是设计团队的工作流。" —— UX Tools 2026春季调研分析

AI设计的终局不是替代

UX Tools 2026年春季调研（1478名设计师参与）显示，53%的团队将"可评估工具的AI生成效率"作为选型核心维度。这说明设计师已经从"AI会不会取代我"的焦虑，转向了"哪个AI工具真正能帮我提效"的务实。

设计工具的AI化不是零和博弈。Figma 2025年收入10.6亿美元（同比+41%），说明即使在竞争加剧的环境下，设计工具市场仍在增长。AI不是在消灭设计师的工作，而是在重新定义设计师的工作——从画框线、对齐像素的"美工"，转向定义设计系统、指导AI、把控品质的"设计导演"。

从Sketch到Figma是一次协作革命，从Figma到AI原生设计工具是一次生产力革命。2026年，这场革命才刚刚进入白热化阶段。

Figma's Counterattack: Code Layer + Agent

On June 25, Figma released perhaps the biggest update in its history. Three core changes deserve attention:

First, the code layer enters the collaboration canvas. Teams can directly clone code repositories within Figma and test flows at the design layer; the wall between design and development is further dismantled. This isn't a simple "design-to-code" plugin -- code itself becomes part of the design canvas, with designers and developers collaborating in the same space.

Second, Design Agent is embedded in the canvas, not a sidebar. This is the most brilliant aspect of Figma's AI strategy. Previous AI design tools (including Figma's own early AI features) placed ChatGPT-like dialog boxes in the sidebar -- you describe a need, AI generates a design, you copy-paste it back. This creates massive "Toggle Tax": frequent switching between design tools and AI tools causes context loss; efficiency drops rather than rises. Design Agent embeds design-system-aware Agents directly in the canvas, with design systems (component libraries, tokens, variables) serving as AI's exclusive context window. The more mature a team's design system, the greater the AI gains.

Third, Parallel Prompting and control priority. Agents can simultaneously generate multiple style/direction options for selection, but AI generation serves only as a starting point -- Figma deliberately emphasizes "humans make the final decisions," preventing AI from overstepping. This is a precise reading of designer psychology: designers don't fear AI helping; they fear AI designing for them.

The Paradigm Shift in Design Tools

The Figma vs Stitch war is essentially a collision of two design tool philosophies.

Stitch represents the "zero barrier" route: enabling people who can't design to create UI with voice. Its target users aren't professional designers, but product managers, entrepreneurs, small business owners -- people with ideas who can't use Figma. Free is its weapon; the Google ecosystem is its distribution channel.

Figma represents the "augment the professional" route: making professional designers and design teams more efficient. Its moat is design systems -- enterprise component libraries, tokens, variables, design specifications. These are organizational assets built over years; the deeper AI understands these assets, the greater the value.

"Stitch wants everyone to be able to design; Figma wants designers to become more powerful. Both directions are valid, but the business logic is completely different -- Stitch sells Google ecosystem stickiness; Figma sells design team workflows." -- UX Tools Spring 2026 survey analysis

The Endgame of AI Design Isn't Replacement

The UX Tools Spring 2026 survey (1,478 designers) shows 53% of teams now rank "evaluable AI generation efficiency of tools" as a core selection criterion. This indicates designers have shifted from anxiety about "will AI replace me" to pragmatism about "which AI tool actually helps me be more efficient."

The AI-ification of design tools isn't a zero-sum game. Figma's 2025 revenue was $1.06 billion (+41% YoY), showing that even amid intensifying competition, the design tools market is still growing. AI isn't eliminating designers' jobs; it's redefining them -- from "artists" who draw boxes and align pixels, to "design directors" who define design systems, guide AI, and control quality.

Sketch to Figma was a collaboration revolution; Figma to AI-native design tools is a productivity revolution. In 2026, this revolution is just heating up.
