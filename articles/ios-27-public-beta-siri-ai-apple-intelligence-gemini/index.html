十年了。Siri终于变聪明了。

7月13日，苹果开放了iOS 27、iPadOS 27、macOS 27 Golden Gate、watchOS 27的首个公开测试版。最受关注的功能没有任何悬念——全新AI版Siri。这不是一次小修小补的升级，而是Siri诞生十四年以来最彻底的一次重构：基于Google Gemini驱动，具备完整的多轮对话能力，可以理解复杂上下文，可以跨App操作，可以生成内容。

换句话说：Siri终于从"定闹钟、查天气、设提醒"的语音指令工具，变成了一个真正的AI助手。

为什么这次Siri升级不一样？

过去几年苹果在AI上的落后是有目共睹的。当ChatGPT已经能写代码、做分析、完成复杂工作流的时候，Siri还在"对不起，我好像不太明白你在说什么"。苹果不是没努力过，但每次AI功能的更新都像挤牙膏——改进一点搜索、加一点自动摘要、开放一点API，但Siri本身始终是那个"不太聪明"的语音助手。

这次不一样。iOS 27里的新Siri有三个本质变化：

第一，从单轮指令到多轮对话。以前你跟Siri说话，它只能处理一条指令，问完就忘。现在它可以像ChatGPT一样进行多轮对话，记住你们之前聊了什么，可以根据上下文澄清问题、修正答案、深入追问。

第二，跨App操作。新Siri可以调用系统级API帮你完成跨App任务——比如"帮我把昨天拍的照片发微信给张三，顺便在日历上记一下明天下午三点开会"。以前你需要打开照片App、分享到微信、找到张三、发送，再打开日历App、新建事件、设置时间——现在一句话搞定。

第三，屏幕内容理解。Siri现在可以理解你当前屏幕上显示的内容——你在看一个网页，可以问"帮我总结一下这篇文章的要点"；你收到一封长邮件，可以说"帮我回复说我同意会议时间"；你在看照片，可以问"这张照片是在哪里拍的"。这是真正的"屏幕级AI助手"体验。

Siri成为AI入口：苹果的防守反击

把Siri升级为AI助手，不只是产品功能更新，更是苹果的战略级防守反击。

过去两年，苹果最大的焦虑是什么？是App生态的入口价值正在被AI蚕食。以前用户想要做什么事，需要打开对应的App——打车打开滴滴、吃饭打开美团、订酒店打开携程。但如果AI助手可以直接帮你完成这些事，用户为什么还要打开App？如果ChatGPT或Gemini成了用户和互联网服务之间的中间层，那苹果精心经营了十五年的App Store生态，价值就会被架空。

"谁控制了AI助手入口，谁就控制了下一代互联网的流量分配权。苹果必须让Siri成为那个入口，而不是让ChatGPT或Gemini在iPhone上取而代之。"—— Dawn Vision编辑部判断

这就是为什么苹果宁愿放下"所有技术自研"的骄傲，选择跟Google合作使用Gemini——因为自研的进度太慢了，如果再等一两年才推出真正可用的AI Siri，用户可能已经习惯了用ChatGPT App或其他第三方AI助手，那时候再想抢回入口就难了。

选择Google而不是OpenAI也是一个耐人寻味的决定。一方面，Google作为搜索引擎巨头，对苹果的威胁相对可控——它不做操作系统、不做硬件，本质上还是一家服务提供商；另一方面，Gemini在多模态和实时性上确实有优势，更适合做系统级助手。

当然，第一个公测版肯定不完美。早期用户反馈显示新Siri还存在反应慢、偶尔理解错误、第三方App支持有限等问题。但方向是对的——Siri终于从一个"鸡肋功能"变成了iOS体验的核心。当十亿iPhone用户开始习惯"有问题问Siri"的时候，苹果就守住了AI时代的入口。

这一仗，苹果输不起。从iOS 27的AI Siri开始，反击正式打响。

Ten years. Siri finally got smart.

On July 13, Apple opened the first public betas of iOS 27, iPadOS 27, macOS 27 Golden Gate, and watchOS 27. The headliner was never in doubt — the all-new AI Siri. This wasn't a incremental upgrade. It was the most fundamental rebuild in Siri's 14-year history: powered by Google Gemini, equipped with full multi-turn conversation, capable of understanding complex context, operating across apps, and generating content.

In other words: Siri finally evolved from a "set alarms, check weather, set reminders" voice command tool into a real AI assistant.

Why This Siri Upgrade Is Different

Apple's AI lag over the past few years was there for all to see. While ChatGPT was writing code, doing analysis, and completing complex workflows, Siri was still stuck on "I'm not sure I understand." It's not that Apple didn't try — every AI update felt incremental: slightly better search, a little auto-summarization, some API openings — but Siri itself remained that "not-too-bright" voice assistant.

This time is different. The new Siri in iOS 27 brings three fundamental changes:

First: from single commands to multi-turn conversation. Previously Siri could only handle one instruction at a time, forgetting after each exchange. Now it can hold multi-turn conversations like ChatGPT, remember what you discussed earlier, clarify questions based on context, correct answers, and follow up.

Second: cross-app operations. The new Siri can invoke system-level APIs to complete cross-app tasks — like "send yesterday's photo to Zhang San on WeChat and put a 3pm meeting tomorrow on my calendar." Previously that required opening Photos, sharing to WeChat, finding the contact, sending; then opening Calendar, creating an event, setting the time — now one sentence handles it.

Third: screen content awareness. Siri can now understand what's currently on your screen — reading a webpage, ask "summarize the key points"; receiving a long email, say "reply that I agree to the meeting time"; viewing a photo, ask "where was this taken?" That's true "screen-level AI assistant" experience.

Siri as AI Gateway: Apple's Defensive Counterattack

Upgrading Siri into an AI assistant isn't just a feature update — it's a strategic counteroffensive for Apple.

What has been Apple's biggest anxiety over the past two years? AI eroding the gateway value of its app ecosystem. Previously, to do anything users opened the corresponding app — Didi for rides, Meituan for food, Ctrip for hotels. But if an AI assistant can handle those tasks directly, why open apps at all? If ChatGPT or Gemini becomes the intermediary between users and internet services, Apple's carefully built 15-year App Store ecosystem gets disintermediated.

"Whoever controls the AI assistant gateway controls traffic distribution for the next generation of the internet. Apple has to make Siri that gateway — not let ChatGPT or Gemini take its place on the iPhone."— Dawn Vision Editorial Assessment

That's why Apple set aside its "build everything in-house" pride and partnered with Google on Gemini — because in-house progress was too slow. Waiting another year or two for a truly capable AI Siri risked users already acclimating to ChatGPT app or third-party assistants, making it much harder to win back the gateway.

Choosing Google over OpenAI is also a telling decision. For one, Google as a search giant is a more controllable threat — it doesn't make operating systems or hardware, remaining fundamentally a service provider. For another, Gemini genuinely has advantages in multimodal and real-time performance, making it better suited for a system-level assistant.

Of course, the first public beta won't be perfect. Early user feedback reports slow responses, occasional comprehension errors, and limited third-party app support. But the direction is right — Siri is finally going from "gimmick feature" to the core of the iOS experience. When a billion iPhone users start habitually asking Siri for things, Apple holds onto the AI-era gateway.

This is a battle Apple can't afford to lose. Starting with iOS 27's AI Siri, the counteroffensive is officially on.
